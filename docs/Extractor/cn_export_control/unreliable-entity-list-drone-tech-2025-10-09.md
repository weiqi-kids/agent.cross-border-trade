# 不可靠实体清单工作机制关于将反无人机技术公司等外国实体列入不可靠实体清单的公告 (Announcement on Adding Counter-Drone Technology Companies and Other Foreign Entities to the Unreliable Entity List)

## Summary

The Unreliable Entity List Working Mechanism announced on October 9, 2025 (Announcement No. 2025-10) the addition of 14 foreign entities and their affiliates to China's Unreliable Entity List, including Dedrone by Axon (counter-drone technology company), TechInsights and its 7 branch offices, BAE Systems, and other US defense technology enterprises.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Unreliable Entity List Working Mechanism (stamped by Ministry of Commerce)
- **Effective Date**: 2025-10-09
- **Scope**: 14 foreign entities and their branch offices, primarily US-based defense and technology companies
- **Legal Basis**: Foreign Trade Law, National Security Law, Anti-Foreign Sanctions Law

## Key Provisions

**Listed Entities** (14 entities including branches):
1. Dedrone by Axon (counter-drone technology company)
2. TechInsights and its 7 branch offices
3. BAE Systems
4. Other US defense technology enterprises (not fully enumerated in summary)

**Three Main Restrictions**:

1. **Import/Export Ban**: "Prohibition on the above entities from engaging in import and export activities related to China"

2. **Investment Ban**: "Prohibition on the above entities from making new investments within Chinese territory"

3. **Transaction and Cooperation Ban**: "Prohibition on organizations and individuals within Chinese territory from engaging in related transactions and cooperation with the above entities, particularly transmission of data and provision of sensitive information to the above entities"

**Enforcement Mechanism**: The announcement is based on China's legal framework for countering foreign entities deemed harmful to national security and interests.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

This enforcement action has immediate and significant supply chain implications:

1. **Direct Impact on Listed Entities**:
   - Complete severance of commercial relationships with Chinese suppliers and customers
   - Inability to source components, materials, or services from China
   - Loss of access to Chinese market for products and services

2. **Sectoral Impact**:
   - **Defense Technology**: Counter-drone systems (Dedrone by Axon) face complete market exclusion
   - **Semiconductor Intelligence**: TechInsights, a leading reverse engineering and analysis firm, loses access to Chinese-manufactured semiconductors for analysis, potentially impacting global chip intelligence
   - **Defense Systems**: BAE Systems' China-related operations and supply chains disrupted

3. **Compliance Burden for Chinese Entities**:
   - Chinese companies must immediately cease all transactions with listed entities
   - Compliance departments must screen these entities from supplier/customer databases
   - Potential legal liability for Chinese entities that continue engagement

4. **Technology Transfer Restrictions**:
   - Explicit ban on data transmission and sensitive information provision creates operational challenges for multinational corporations with Chinese subsidiaries
   - Technical support, maintenance, and R&D collaboration affected

5. **Investment Restrictions**:
   - Listed entities cannot establish new operations, joint ventures, or investments in China
   - Potential implications for existing investments unclear from announcement

6. **Indirect Effects**:
   - Foreign companies may implement "de-risking" strategies, avoiding entities with ties to listed companies
   - Supply chain diversification accelerates as companies seek to reduce dependency on potentially restricted entities

7. **Retaliatory Context**:
   - This action appears to be part of ongoing trade and technology tensions
   - Companies should anticipate potential additions to the list in response to foreign government actions
