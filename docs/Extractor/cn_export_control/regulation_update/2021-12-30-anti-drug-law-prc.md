## Summary

The Anti-Drug Law of the People's Republic of China (中华人民共和国禁毒法) was enacted on December 29, 2007 by the 31st Session of the Standing Committee of the 10th National People's Congress and came into force on June 1, 2008. The law establishes a comprehensive national framework for anti-narcotics governance, covering the prohibition of cultivation, production, trafficking, and use of controlled substances, as well as the regulation of precursor chemicals critical to drug manufacturing. Its inclusion on the MOFCOM Export Control Information Network underscores the relevance of precursor chemical controls to China's broader export control regime.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Standing Committee of the National People's Congress (全国人民代表大会常务委员会)
- **Effective Date**: 2008-06-01 (enacted 2007-12-29; indexed on MOFCOM portal 2021-12-30)
- **Scope**: Nationwide; applies to cultivation, production, trade, transportation, and use of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances, as well as the production and export of precursor chemicals (易制毒化学品)

## Key Provisions

- **Controlled Substances Definition**: Defines controlled substances as opium, heroin, methamphetamine (ice/冰毒), morphine, cannabis, cocaine, and all other narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances regulated by the state that can cause addiction.
- **Governance Framework**: Establishes the "prevention as the primary focus, integrated governance" (预防为主、综合治理) principle; creates the National Anti-Drug Committee (国家禁毒委员会) to coordinate national efforts.
- **Prohibition Measures**:
  - Prohibits illegal cultivation of narcotic plant sources (罂粟等毒品原植物)
  - Implements licensing and inspection systems for entities handling narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances
  - Controls the production and trade of precursor chemicals (易制毒化学品); exporters of such chemicals must obtain relevant permits
  - Authorizes border searches and customs inspections for drug interdiction
- **Treatment and Rehabilitation**:
  - Mandates addiction treatment programs for dependent users
  - Establishes community-based rehabilitation (社区康复, 3-year duration)
  - Provides for compulsory isolation treatment (强制隔离戒毒, standard 2-year period)
  - Protects rehabilitated individuals from employment and educational discrimination
- **International Cooperation**: Provides a legal basis for transnational drug enforcement collaboration and asset-sharing from criminal proceeds; facilitates cooperation with international anti-drug bodies.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*The following is speculative analysis for supply chain risk reference only.*

- **Precursor Chemical Exporters**: Companies exporting precursor chemicals (e.g., ephedrine, acetic anhydride, potassium permanganate) subject to Chinese export controls under this law face licensing requirements. Non-compliance may result in shipment delays, permit revocation, or enforcement actions.
- **Pharmaceutical and Chemical Industries**: Manufacturers of dual-use chemicals that could be diverted for drug production must maintain robust compliance programs to avoid inadvertent violations of precursor chemical provisions.
- **Cross-Border Trade Scrutiny**: Customs authorities are empowered to inspect and detain shipments suspected of containing controlled substances or undeclared precursor chemicals, potentially affecting transit times for legitimate chemical trade.
- **Indirect Link to Export Control Regime**: The law's precursor chemical controls overlap with China's broader export control framework (出口管制法); companies should monitor both regimes for controlled item listings.
