## Summary

MOFCOM Order 2025 No. 7 suspends, for one year, the countermeasures imposed under MOFCOM Order 2025 No. 6 against five US-related subsidiaries of Hanwha Ocean Co., Ltd. (韩华海洋株式会社). The suspension is a reciprocal response to the United States suspending, for one year from 2025-11-10, its Section 301 investigation measures targeting China's maritime, logistics, and shipbuilding sectors. The decision is grounded in the Anti-Foreign Sanctions Law of the PRC and its implementing provisions.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce of the PRC, Security and Control Bureau (中华人民共和国商务部安全与管制局)
- **Effective Date**: 2025-11-10 (生效日期 2025年11月10日)
- **Scope**: Five US-related subsidiaries of Hanwha Ocean Co., Ltd.; suspension duration of one year

## Key Provisions

- **Core decision**: Suspend the measures under MOFCOM Order 2025 No. 6 (商务部令二〇二五年第6号) for one year (暂停……相关措施一年) as applied to the 5 US-related subsidiaries of Hanwha Ocean (韩华海洋株式会社).
- **Trigger/reason**: The US, from 2025-11-10, suspended for one year its Section 301 investigation measures against China's maritime, logistics, and shipbuilding industries; China responded reciprocally.
- **Suspension term**: One (1) year.
- **Legal basis**: 《中华人民共和国反外国制裁法》(Anti-Foreign Sanctions Law of the PRC) and related implementing provisions.
- **Subsidiary names**: The exact names of the 5 subsidiaries are not enumerated in this Order; they are referenced from the underlying MOFCOM Order 2025 No. 6.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*Speculative analysis.* This reciprocal one-year suspension de-escalates the US-China shipbuilding/maritime dispute and temporarily restores normal commercial access for the five Hanwha Ocean US-affiliated subsidiaries vis-à-vis Chinese counterparties. Shipbuilding, marine equipment, and logistics supply chains linked to these entities gain a one-year window of reduced transaction risk. However, the time-bound nature (one year, contingent on continued US suspension of Section 301 measures) means the relief is conditional and reversible, so firms should treat it as a temporary détente rather than a permanent settlement.
