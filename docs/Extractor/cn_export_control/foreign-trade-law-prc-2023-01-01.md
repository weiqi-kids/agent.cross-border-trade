## Summary

The Foreign Trade Law of the People's Republic of China establishes the comprehensive legal framework governing all foreign trade activities, including goods, technology, and services trade. Last revised on December 30, 2022, this fundamental legislation outlines operator requirements, trade remedies, IP protection, investigation powers, and legal liabilities for violations.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: National People's Congress Standing Committee (全国人民代表大会常务委员会)
- **Effective Date**: July 1, 2004 (with subsequent revisions on November 7, 2016 and December 30, 2022)
- **Scope**: All trade activities involving goods, technology, and international services between China and foreign entities

## Key Provisions

### 1. Foreign Trade Operators (Chapter 2)
- Defines qualification requirements for entities engaged in import/export activities
- Establishes registration and licensing systems for trade operators
- Outlines rights and obligations of foreign trade practitioners

### 2. Goods and Technology Trade (Chapter 3)
- Implements permit and quota systems for restricted items
- Establishes import/export prohibition mechanisms for national security concerns
- Covers nuclear materials, weapons-related goods, and military equipment
- Regulates agricultural, mineral, and natural resource exports

### 3. International Services Trade (Chapter 4)
- Provides market access framework for cross-border services
- Establishes national treatment principles with specified exceptions
- Regulates services related to national security and defense

### 4. Intellectual Property Protection (Chapter 5)
- Prohibits import/export of IP-infringing goods
- Empowers customs to detain suspected infringing products
- Establishes liability for IP violations in trade contexts

### 5. Trade Remedies and Investigation (Chapters 7-8)
- Authorizes anti-dumping, anti-subsidy, and safeguard measures
- Grants investigation powers to assess trade impacts
- Establishes procedures for responding to foreign trade restrictions
- Enables retaliatory measures against discriminatory practices

### 6. Legal Liability (Chapter 10)
- Specifies penalties for illegal trade activities including fines (RMB 50,000-500,000)
- Provides for operational license suspension or revocation
- Addresses fraudulent practices, smuggling facilitation, and regulatory violations

5. **IP Enforcement Enhancement**: Stronger customs detention powers for IP-infringing goods increase seizure risks for companies with complex manufacturing networks, particularly in consumer electronics and pharmaceuticals.
