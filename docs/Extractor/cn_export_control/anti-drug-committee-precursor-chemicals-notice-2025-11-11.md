# 国家禁毒委员会办公室关于防止制毒物品和非列管化学品及设备流失的提醒通知 (Notice on Preventing Loss of Drug Manufacturing Materials and Non-Listed Chemical Products and Equipment)

## Summary
The National Anti-Drug Committee Office issued a comprehensive notice reminding enterprises and individuals of their obligations to prevent illegal diversion of drug precursor chemicals and manufacturing equipment. This guidance addresses control measures for both listed and non-listed precursor chemicals, emphasizing criminal liability, tax compliance, customs enforcement, and international cooperation requirements.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: National Anti-Drug Committee Office (国家禁毒委员会办公室)
- **Effective Date**: 2025-11-11
- **Scope**: Enterprises and individuals involved in production, sale, transport, import/export of drug precursor chemicals and equipment; network operators; postal and logistics companies; international freight forwarders

## Key Provisions

**1. Criminal Liability Framework**
- Prohibition on illegal production, sale, and transport of listed precursors (acetic anhydride, ether, etc.)
- Criminal liability extends to non-listed precursor chemicals when used to assist drug manufacturing
- Applies to both domestic and cross-border violations

**2. Compliance Obligations**
- Tax compliance requirements for enterprises producing precursor materials
- Customs smuggling enforcement and penalties
- Anti-money laundering and foreign exchange compliance obligations
- Internal management guidelines for legitimate producers and exporters

**3. Network Operator Responsibilities**
- Monitoring obligations for prohibited content
- Internet publication restrictions and real-name registration requirements
- Platform accountability for preventing illegal transactions

**4. Logistics and Transportation Controls**
- Reporting obligations for postal, courier, and logistics enterprises
- International freight forwarder accountability
- Enhanced inspection requirements for suspicious shipments

**5. International Compliance**
- Risk awareness regarding international regulations, particularly in high-risk countries
- Access to legal assistance through Chinese diplomatic missions abroad
- Coordination with foreign customs and enforcement agencies

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

**High-Risk Sectors:**
- Chemical manufacturers producing legitimate industrial products with dual-use potential
- International logistics companies handling chemical shipments to sensitive jurisdictions
- E-commerce platforms facilitating chemical product sales
- Pharmaceutical companies using precursor chemicals in production

**Compliance Requirements:**
- Enhanced due diligence on end-users and end-use applications
- Robust internal control systems for tracking production, storage, and disposal
- Real-name verification systems for online transactions
- Mandatory reporting systems for suspicious activities

**Cross-Border Trade Implications:**
- Increased customs scrutiny for chemical exports to high-risk countries
- Potential delays in clearance procedures requiring additional documentation
- Enhanced coordination between Chinese and foreign enforcement agencies
- Legal risks for freight forwarders and logistics providers in supply chain
