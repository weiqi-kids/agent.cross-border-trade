## Summary
Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI) issued a public comment notice on October 3, 2024, for proposed revisions to export control regulations. The revisions add specific perfluorocarboxylic acids and related substances as Class 1 Special Chemical Substances, following amendments to Japan's Chemical Substance Examination and Manufacturing Control Law.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: Japan Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI)
- **Effective Date**: To be determined (currently in public comment period)
- **Scope**: Export controls on perfluorocarboxylic acids (8-carbon branched structures) and related PFAS compounds

## Key Provisions
- **Legal Basis**: Revisions follow July 10, 2024 amendments to Chemical Substance Examination and Manufacturing Control Law
- **Designated Chemicals**: Class 1 Special Chemical Substances added to export controls include:
  - **Perfluorocarboxylic acids** with branched structures containing 8 carbons and their salts
  - **Perfluorooctanoic acid (PFOA) related substances**:
    - Perfluorooctyl iodide
    - 8:2 fluorotelomer alcohol
    - Compounds containing pentadecafluoroalkyl groups that generate perfluorooctanoic acid through natural degradation
- **Public Comment Period**: October 3 – November 1, 2024
- **Regulatory Framework**: Amendment to goods designated under Export Trade Control Order Article 4, Section 2
