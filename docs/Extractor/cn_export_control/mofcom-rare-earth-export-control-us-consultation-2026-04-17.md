# 商务部回应稀土出口管制：中美将通过经贸磋商机制保持沟通（MOFCOM Response on Rare Earth Export Controls: China-US to Maintain Communication via Trade Consultation Mechanism）

## Summary

China's Ministry of Commerce confirmed that export control measures announced on October 9, 2025—related to rare earth materials—will be suspended until November 10, 2026, following consensus reached during China-US trade consultations in Kuala Lumpur. Ministry spokesperson He Yadong stated that both nations will continue to maintain communication through the established trade consultation mechanism regarding their respective concerns. The announcement signals a temporary de-escalation in the rare earth export control standoff between the two countries.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: China Ministry of Commerce (MOFCOM), spokesperson He Yadong
- **Effective Date**: Suspension effective through November 10, 2026 (original measures announced October 9, 2025)
- **Scope**: Rare earth export control measures; bilateral China-US trade relations

## Key Provisions

- Rare earth export control measures announced October 9, 2025 are suspended until November 10, 2026
- Suspension is contingent on the consensus reached at Kuala Lumpur China-US trade consultations
- China and the US will continue communication through the trade consultation mechanism on respective concerns
- Statement made at regular MOFCOM press briefing on April 9, 2026 (news conference date)

The temporary suspension of rare earth export controls provides short-term relief to global supply chains dependent on Chinese rare earth materials, particularly in defense, electronics, electric vehicles, and clean energy sectors.
