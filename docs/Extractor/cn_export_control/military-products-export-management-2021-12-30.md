## Summary
China's Military Products Export Management Regulations establish a licensing system for military equipment, production facilities, materials, technologies, and related services. Only licensed military trading companies may conduct exports, and all transactions require state approval within 20 days of contract submission. The regulations implement three core principles: supporting legitimate self-defense, preventing harm to peace and stability, and avoiding interference in internal affairs.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: State Council and Central Military Commission
- **Effective Date**: January 1, 1998 (revised October 15, 2002)
- **Scope**: Military equipment, specialized production facilities, materials, technologies, and related services listed in Military Products Export Management Catalog

## Key Provisions

### Core Principles
1. Support legitimate self-defense capabilities of recipient nations
2. Prevent harm to regional and global peace, security, and stability
3. Avoid interference in recipient nations' internal affairs

### Management System
- **Licensing Requirement**: All exports operate under mandatory licensing system requiring approval before execution
- **Authorized Entities**: Only licensed military trading companies may conduct export operations; individuals are prohibited
- **Control List**: Military Products Export Management Catalog defines controlled items

### Approval Process
- Military trading companies must apply to the state military products export authority within 20 days of contract submission
- Approval required before transactions become effective
- Export permits must be obtained before shipment
- Customs clearance dependent on valid certificates

### Documentation Requirements
- Contract submission and approval within 20-day window
- Export permits from state authority
- Valid certificates for customs clearance

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

**Scope**: Affects all trade in military equipment, defense technologies, specialized military production facilities, and related services.

**Impact on Supply Chains**:
- **Exclusive Market Access**: Only state-licensed military trading companies can export, effectively creating government-controlled monopoly
- **Complex Approval Process**: 20-day application window plus approval time extends transaction timelines significantly
- **Policy Discretion**: Three core principles (self-defense, stability, non-interference) provide broad discretionary authority to deny licenses
- **End-Use Restrictions**: Exports implicitly tied to recipient nation's defense posture and relationship with China
- **Documentation Chain**: Multi-stage approval (contract → export authority → permit → customs) creates multiple control points

**Strategic Considerations**:
- Military products trade functions as extension of foreign policy, not purely commercial activity
- Private companies cannot directly access Chinese military technologies or equipment
- Principle-based approval system allows for flexible interpretation based on geopolitical considerations
- Catalog-based control system enables granular item-by-item restrictions
- Joint State Council/Central Military Commission authority indicates military's direct role in export decisions
