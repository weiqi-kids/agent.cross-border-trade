## Summary

On March 2, 2026, China's Ministry of Commerce spokesperson responded to the UK's February 24 announcement of a new round of Russia-related sanctions targeting multiple Chinese enterprises. China strongly opposes the UK's unilateral sanctions, stating they lack a basis in international law and have not been authorized by the United Nations. The spokesperson urged the UK to immediately rescind the sanctions against Chinese entities, warning that China will take necessary measures to protect the legitimate rights and interests of Chinese companies.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce of the People's Republic of China, News Affairs Office (商务部新闻办公室)
- **Effective Date**: 2026-03-02 (spokesperson statement date); UK sanctions announced 2026-02-24
- **Scope**: Multiple Chinese enterprises targeted by UK Russia-related sanctions; China's dual-use export controls and normal China-Russia business interactions

## Key Provisions

- The UK announced a new round of Russia-related sanctions on February 24, 2026, targeting multiple Chinese enterprises.
- China's position:
  1. The UK sanctions are unilateral measures with no basis in international law and no UN authorization.
  2. China strongly objects and is firmly opposed to the UK's actions.
  3. On the Ukraine crisis, China consistently and strictly controls exports of dual-use items in accordance with laws and regulations.
  4. Normal business exchanges and cooperation between Chinese and Russian enterprises should not be interfered with or affected.
- China's demands:
  - The UK should immediately correct its erroneous actions and rescind sanctions on the relevant Chinese entities, in the interest of maintaining good momentum in UK-China relations.
- China's warning:
  - China will take necessary measures to resolutely safeguard the legitimate and lawful rights and interests of Chinese enterprises.
