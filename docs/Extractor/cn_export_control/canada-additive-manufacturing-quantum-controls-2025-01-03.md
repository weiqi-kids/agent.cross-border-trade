# 加拿大将对增材制造等物项出口实施管制(Canada Implements Export Controls on Additive Manufacturing and Quantum Computing Items)

## Summary
Canada's Department of Global Affairs announced new export controls on strategic items including quantum computing components, EUV lithography materials, additive manufacturing equipment, and high-temperature coating technologies, effective 30 days after publication in Part II of the Canada Gazette on December 21, 2024.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: Department of Global Affairs Canada
- **Effective Date**: 30 days after publication in Canada Gazette Part II (published December 21, 2024)
- **Scope**: Export controls on emerging technologies critical to quantum computing, advanced semiconductor manufacturing, and advanced materials

## Key Provisions
**Quantum Computing Controls:**
- Cryogenic refrigeration systems
- Parametric signal amplifiers
- Isotopically enriched silicon and germanium
- Cryogenic wafer probing stations

**Integrated Circuit Manufacturing Controls:**
- Extreme ultraviolet (EUV) masks, reticles, and pellicles used in EUV lithography production
- Critical components for next-generation semiconductor manufacturing

**Additive Manufacturing:**
- Additive manufacturing equipment (specific parameters not detailed in summary)

**Advanced Materials:**
- High-temperature coating development technologies
- Specifically: coatings designed for "corrosion resistance of ceramic matrix composites at temperatures above 1100°C"

**Legal Basis:**
- Amendment to Export Control List under Canada's export control regulations
- Part of strategic technology protection framework

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment
**Quantum Technology Sector:**
- Canada is emerging quantum research hub; these controls may affect international collaborations
- Cryogenic equipment and enriched isotope controls impact quantum computer development supply chains
- Research institutions and startups must navigate export licensing for international partnerships

**Semiconductor Manufacturing:**
- EUV pellicle and mask controls target cutting-edge chip production (sub-7nm processes)
- Affects Canadian suppliers to global semiconductor manufacturing equipment supply chain
- Aligns with allied nations' restrictions on advanced chipmaking technology

**Advanced Manufacturing:**
- Additive manufacturing controls reflect concern over 3D printing of strategic components
- High-temperature coating restrictions protect hypersonic and advanced aerospace technologies
- Ceramic matrix composites used in jet engines, missiles, and hypersonic vehicles

**Strategic Alignment:**
- Coordinates with U.S., EU, and Five Eyes partners on technology export controls
- Strengthens multilateral approach to emerging technology governance
- May signal further controls on AI hardware and other strategic technologies

**Compliance Considerations:**
- Canadian exporters must implement licensing procedures within 30-day window
- International buyers of Canadian quantum and semiconductor equipment should verify license requirements
- Research collaborations involving controlled items require export permits
