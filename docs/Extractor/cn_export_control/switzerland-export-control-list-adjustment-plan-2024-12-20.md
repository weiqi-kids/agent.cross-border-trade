## Summary
Switzerland's Federal Council authorized its Ministry of Economic Affairs, Education and Research (WBF) on December 13, 2024, to submit a proposal by end of 2025 to transfer relevant dual-use items into existing control lists under the Goods Control Ordinance, aiming to align with international standards amid tightening geopolitical tensions.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: Swiss Federal Council and Ministry of Economic Affairs, Education and Research (WBF)
- **Effective Date**: Proposal to be submitted by end of 2025
- **Scope**: Dual-use items in emerging technology sectors requiring enhanced export controls

## Key Provisions
**Authorization and Timeline:**
- Federal Council decision made on December 13, 2024
- WBF authorized to submit proposal to Federal Council by end of 2025
- Proposal will transfer relevant dual-use items into existing control lists under the Goods Control Ordinance

**Policy Background:**
- Geopolitical tensions increasingly affecting export controls on emerging technology dual-use items
- Multiple countries participating in international export control regimes have agreed to or are updating control measures
- Updates aim to accommodate technological advancement

**Policy Objectives:**
- Ensure Swiss export control policy aligns with international standards
- Enable emerging and future technologies to be incorporated into export control regulatory framework
- Maintain Switzerland's coordination with partner nations on strategic technology governance

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment
**Timing Considerations:**
- Proposal phase extends through 2025, providing planning window for affected businesses
- Implementation timeline unclear but likely extends into 2026 or beyond
- Companies should monitor WBF announcements for specific item lists

**Strategic Implications:**
- Switzerland's neutral stance historically allowed flexible trade policies; this signals alignment with Western export control tightening
- Swiss-based technology firms and research institutions may face new licensing requirements
- Supply chains routing through Switzerland for dual-use items should prepare for potential disruptions

**Affected Sectors (Anticipated):**
- Emerging technologies including semiconductors, quantum computing, AI hardware
- Advanced manufacturing equipment
- Cybersecurity and surveillance technologies
