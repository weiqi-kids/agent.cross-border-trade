# 商务部公告2025年第35号 公布将8家台湾地区实体列入出口管制管控名单（MOFCOM Announcement No.35 of 2025 — Adding Eight Taiwan-Region Entities to the Export Control List）

## Summary

China's Ministry of Commerce issued Announcement No.35 of 2025, adding eight Taiwan-region entities to the export control list (出口管制管控名单). Exporting dual-use items to these eight entities is prohibited, ongoing related export activities must cease immediately, and exceptional exports require explicit MOFCOM approval. The action is framed as safeguarding national security and fulfilling non-proliferation obligations.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Security and Control Bureau, Ministry of Commerce (商务部安全与管制局)
- **Effective Date**: Upon publication, 2025-07-09
- **Scope**: Aerospace, defense research, and shipbuilding entities in Taiwan; controls apply to dual-use items (两用物项 / dual-use items)

## Key Provisions

- **Legal basis**: 《中华人民共和国出口管制法》(Export Control Law of the PRC) and 《中华人民共和国两用物项出口管制条例》(Regulations on Export Control of Dual-Use Items of the PRC).
- **Eight listed entities**:
  1. Aerospace Industrial Development Corporation (汉翔航空工业)
  2. GEOSAT Aerospace & Technology Inc.
  3. National Chung-Shan Institute of Science & Technology (中山科学研究院)
  4. JC Technology Inc.
  5. CSBC Corporation, Taiwan (台湾国际造船)
  6. Jong Shyn Shipbuilding Co., Ltd
  7. Lungteh Shipbuilding Co., Ltd
  8. Gong Wei Co., Ltd
- **Control measures**:
  - Prohibition on exporting dual-use items to the eight listed Taiwan entities.
  - Ongoing related export activities must cease immediately.
  - Exports in special circumstances require approval from the Ministry of Commerce.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*The following is speculative analysis.* The listing restricts the eight Taiwan defense/aerospace/shipbuilding entities from sourcing Chinese-origin dual-use items, components and materials. This may disrupt their access to mainland-supplied inputs and tighten cross-strait technology flows in sensitive sectors. Suppliers and intermediaries with mainland China nexus face compliance obligations to screen against the list before fulfilling orders to these entities.
