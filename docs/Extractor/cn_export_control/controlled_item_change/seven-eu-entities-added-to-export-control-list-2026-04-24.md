# 商务部新闻发言人就将7家欧盟实体列入出口管制管控名单答记者问（MOFCOM Spokesperson Answers Press Questions on Adding 7 EU Entities to the Export Control List）

## Summary

China's Ministry of Commerce added seven EU entities (including Herstal Company / 赫斯塔尔公司) to its export control list pursuant to the Export Control Law and the Dual-Use Items Export Control Regulations, citing their involvement in arms sales to Taiwan or collusion with Taiwan. Chinese exporters are prohibited from exporting dual-use items to these entities, and overseas parties may not transfer or provide China-origin dual-use items to them. MOFCOM emphasized the measure targets only a small number of EU military-related entities and will not affect normal China-EU trade.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: China Ministry of Commerce (商务部) / Spokesperson (新闻发言人)
- **Effective Date**: Announcement issued 2026-04-24
- **Scope**: Seven EU military-related entities (含 Herstal / 赫斯塔尔公司); dual-use items (两用物项); China-origin dual-use items held overseas

## Key Provisions

- **Action**: Seven EU entities added to China's export control list (出口管制管控名单).
- **Legal basis**: "根据《中华人民共和国出口管制法》和《中华人民共和国两用物项出口管制条例》" (pursuant to the Export Control Law of the PRC and the Dual-Use Items Export Control Regulations of the PRC).
- **Reason**: The listed entities "曾参与对台军售或与台勾连" (previously participated in arms sales to Taiwan or colluded with Taiwan).
- **Restrictions**:
  - Exporters within China are prohibited from exporting dual-use items to the listed entities.
  - Overseas organizations and individuals are prohibited from transferring or providing China-origin dual-use items to these entities.
- **Scope clarification**: MOFCOM stressed the measure targets only "少数欧盟涉军实体" (a small number of EU military-related entities) and affects only dual-use items, asserting it will not disrupt normal China-EU trade.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*The following is speculative analysis.*

The listing restricts dual-use flows to the seven named EU defense-related entities and to any overseas party seeking to supply them with China-origin dual-use items. Firms in defense and dual-use supply chains with ties to these entities (notably Herstal, a firearms/defense manufacturer) face compliance exposure and potential disruption. The narrow scoping suggests limited spillover to broader China-EU commercial trade, but it reinforces Taiwan-related arms sales as a trigger for Chinese export-control listings.
