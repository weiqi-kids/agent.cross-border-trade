# 商务部公告2025年第21号 公布将16家美国实体列入出口管制管控名单（MOFCOM Announcement No.21 of 2025 — Adding Sixteen US Entities to the Export Control List）

## Summary

China's Ministry of Commerce issued Announcement No.21 of 2025, adding sixteen US entities to the export control list (出口管制管控名单). Exporting dual-use items to these sixteen entities is prohibited, ongoing related export activities must cease immediately, and any exceptional exports require MOFCOM approval. The action cites national security protection and fulfillment of non-proliferation obligations.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Security and Control Bureau, Ministry of Commerce (商务部安全与管制局)
- **Effective Date**: 2025-04-04 (upon publication)
- **Scope**: Sixteen US aerospace, defense, marine and logistics-technology entities; controls apply to dual-use items (两用物项 / dual-use items)

## Key Provisions

- **Legal basis**: 《中华人民共和国出口管制法》(Export Control Law of the PRC) and 《中华人民共和国两用物项出口管制条例》(Regulations on Export Control of Dual-Use Items of the PRC).
- **Sixteen listed US entities**:
  1. High Point Aerotechnologies
  2. Universal Logistics Holdings, Inc.
  3. Source Intelligence, Inc.
  4. Coalition For A Prosperous America
  5. Sierra Nevada Corporation
  6. Edge Autonomy Operations LLC
  7. Cyberlux Corporation
  8. Hudson Technologies Co.
  9. Saronic Technologies, Inc.
  10. Oceaneering International, Inc.
  11. Stick Rudder Enterprises LLC
  12. Cubic Corporation
  13. S3 AeroDefense
  14. TCOM, Limited Partnership
  15. TextOre
  16. ACT1 Federal
- **Control measures**:
  - Prohibition on exporting dual-use items to the sixteen listed US entities.
  - Ongoing related export activities must cease immediately.
  - Exports in special circumstances require approval from the Ministry of Commerce.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*The following is speculative analysis.* The listing cuts the sixteen US entities — spanning aerospace, defense, marine technology and logistics — off from Chinese-origin dual-use inputs. Issued at the height of the April 2025 US-China tariff confrontation, it forms part of a coordinated countermeasure package alongside the Unreliable Entity List additions and rare-earth controls. Several of these entities (e.g., Sierra Nevada Corporation, Cyberlux, Edge Autonomy, Hudson Technologies) also appear on the Unreliable Entity List, compounding their China-market restrictions.
