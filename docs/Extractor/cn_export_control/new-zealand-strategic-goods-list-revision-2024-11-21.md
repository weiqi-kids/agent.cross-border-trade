## Summary
New Zealand's Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade approved updates to the New Zealand Strategic Goods List (NZSGL) on November 12, 2024, incorporating 278 revisions including 47 substantive changes that add new controlled items in semiconductor and quantum computing technologies.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: New Zealand Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade
- **Effective Date**: 2024-11-12
- **Scope**: National export control list covering strategic goods including emerging technologies

## Key Provisions
The revision includes:
- **278 total updates** to the strategic goods list
  - **231 revisions** involve clarifications and editorial adjustments that do not change existing control scope
  - **47 revisions** involve substantive changes including additions, deletions, or modifications of controlled items

**Newly Controlled Items:**
- Semiconductor technology items
- Quantum computing technology items

The updates align New Zealand's export controls with international standards and address emerging technologies requiring strategic oversight.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment
**Impact on Semiconductor Supply Chains:**
- New Zealand suppliers and exporters of semiconductor equipment, materials, or technology must now comply with enhanced controls
- Companies trading semiconductor items with New Zealand partners should verify compliance requirements

**Impact on Quantum Technology Supply Chains:**
- First-time addition of quantum computing controls may affect research collaborations and technology transfers
- Organizations developing or exporting quantum technologies need to assess license requirements

**Strategic Considerations:**
- Reflects Five Eyes alignment on emerging technology controls
- May signal coordinated approach among allied nations for semiconductor and quantum technology export restrictions
- Companies with New Zealand operations should review their product portfolios against the updated list
