## Summary

China's Ministry of Commerce issued Announcement No. 11 of 2026, adding 20 Japanese entities involved in enhancing Japan's military capabilities to the Export Control List (管控名单). The announcement prohibits Chinese exporters from exporting dual-use items to these entities and prohibits overseas organizations/individuals from transferring China-origin dual-use items to them. This represents a major escalation in China-Japan export control tensions, effective immediately from the date of publication (2026-02-24).

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce, Bureau of Safety and Security Control (安全与管制局)
- **Effective Date**: 2026-02-24 (effective upon publication)
- **Scope**: 20 Japanese entities in defense, aerospace, and military technology sectors
- **Legal Basis**: Export Control Law of the PRC (《中华人民共和国出口管制法》) and Regulations on Export Control of Dual-Use Items (《中华人民共和国两用物项出口管制条例》)

## Key Provisions

1. **Export Ban**: All Chinese exporters are prohibited from exporting dual-use items to the 20 listed entities
2. **Re-transfer Ban**: Overseas organizations and individuals are prohibited from transferring or providing China-origin dual-use items to the listed entities
3. **Immediate Cessation**: Ongoing related activities must stop immediately
4. **Special Licensing Exception**: In special circumstances where export is necessary, exporters may apply to MOFCOM for individual approval

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

**Note: The following impact assessment contains speculative analysis based on the policy content.**

1. **Defense & Aerospace Supply Chains**: Major Japanese defense contractors (Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, Kawasaki Heavy Industries, IHI) are directly impacted, affecting sourcing of Chinese-origin dual-use materials and components
2. **Semiconductor & Electronics**: Fujitsu Defense and NEC subsidiaries are listed, potentially affecting advanced electronics supply chains
3. **Shipbuilding**: Mitsubishi Shipbuilding and Japan Marine United are key naval vessel builders — Chinese materials for ship construction may be restricted
4. **Research & Development**: JAXA and the National Defense Academy being listed signals China's targeting of Japan's military R&D pipeline
5. **Broader Implications**: This is part of China's counter-measures against Japan's re-militarization, and may trigger further reciprocal actions
6. **Compliance Burden**: Chinese exporters dealing with any Japanese entities must now verify they are not on the control list before proceeding with dual-use item exports
