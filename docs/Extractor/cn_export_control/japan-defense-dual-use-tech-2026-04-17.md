## Summary

Japan's Defense Minister Koizumi Shinjiro announced plans to strengthen dual-use technology development, stating in parliamentary debate that civilian and defense innovations are "inseparable" and increasingly difficult to distinguish. Japan is actively upgrading its military capabilities—targeting 2% of GDP in defense spending by 2027—with emphasis on applying civilian dual-use technologies such as AI, drones, and space/cyber capabilities to military purposes.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Japan Ministry of Defense (Japanese Defense Minister Koizumi Shinjiro)

## Key Provisions

- Japan plans to accelerate dual-use technology R&D, blurring the line between civilian and defense applications
- Defense technologies will be commercialized as competitive products; advanced civilian technologies will be adapted for defense use
- Defense spending to increase to 2% of GDP by 2027
- Priority areas: AI, unmanned systems (drones), space capabilities, and cyber capabilities
- Integration with U.S. military systems maintained as a strategic priority
- Emphasis on strike capability development alongside dual-use technology programs

- **Export control risk**: Japan is likely to align more closely with U.S. export control frameworks (e.g., Entity List, ITAR) for dual-use technology, increasing compliance burdens for cross-border trade involving Japan
