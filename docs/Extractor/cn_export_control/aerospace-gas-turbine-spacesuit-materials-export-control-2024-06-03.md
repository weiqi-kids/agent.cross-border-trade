## Summary
The Ministry of Commerce, General Administration of Customs, and Central Military Commission Equipment Development Department issued Announcement 2024 No. 21, implementing export controls on four categories of strategic materials effective July 1, 2024. The controlled items include aerospace manufacturing equipment, gas turbine blade production technology, spacesuit visor molds, and ultra-high molecular weight polyethylene fiber meeting specific performance specifications.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce, General Administration of Customs, Central Military Commission Equipment Development Department (three-agency joint announcement)
- **Effective Date**: 2024-07-01
- **Scope**: Exporters of aerospace structures manufacturing equipment, gas turbine engine production equipment, spacesuit visor manufacturing molds, ultra-high molecular weight polyethylene fiber (strength ≥40cN/dtex), and related technologies

## Key Provisions

**Category 1: Aerospace Structures & Engines**
- Manufacturing equipment for titanium/aluminum superplastic forming and diffusion bonding in aircraft or spacecraft components
- Related software and technical documentation
- Specialized tooling for aerospace structural component production

**Category 2: Gas Turbine Engine Equipment**
- Single-crystal casting equipment for turbine blade production
- Precision casting intermediates and specialized tooling
- Related software and technical specifications
- Manufacturing technology for high-performance turbine components

**Category 3: Spacesuit Visor Equipment**
- Specialized molds for manufacturing spacesuits
- Related technical materials and specifications
- Production documentation for visor components

**Category 4: Ultra-High Molecular Weight Polyethylene Fiber**
- Fibers meeting strength specifications (≥40cN/dtex) in untwisted condition
- Composite materials with documented ballistic performance
- Production technology and documentation
- Manufacturing processes for high-strength synthetic fibers

**Export Process Requirements:**
Exporters must obtain permits through provincial commerce departments by submitting:
- Export contracts
- Technical specifications or inspection reports
- End-user certifications
- Importer background information
- Identity documentation of authorized representatives

**Approval Authority:**
- Ministry of Commerce processes applications within statutory timeframes
- Permits issued after comprehensive review
- State Council approval required for exports significantly impacting national security

**Enforcement:**
- Unauthorized exports face administrative penalties
- Potential criminal prosecution for serious violations
- Customs enforcement at port of exit

**Legal Basis:**
- Export Control Law of the People's Republic of China
- State Council and Central Military Commission authorization
- National security and international non-proliferation obligations

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

**Critical Technologies Affected:**
- **Aerospace Manufacturing:** Advanced forming and bonding equipment essential for aircraft structural components
- **Gas Turbine Production:** Single-crystal casting technology critical for high-efficiency turbine blades in aviation and power generation
- **Space Exploration:** Spacesuit visor manufacturing capabilities for manned space missions
- **Ballistic Materials:** High-strength polyethylene fiber used in body armor, helmets, and protective equipment

**Industry Sectors Impacted:**
- Commercial aviation manufacturers relying on advanced forming equipment
- Gas turbine suppliers for power generation and propulsion systems
- Defense contractors using ballistic fiber composites
- Research institutions developing aerospace materials and processes

**Strategic Implications:**
- Restricts transfer of dual-use aerospace manufacturing capabilities
- Protects China's technological advantages in single-crystal turbine blade production
- Safeguards military-critical ballistic material supply chains
- Supports domestic aerospace industry development by limiting foreign competition access to advanced equipment

**International Trade Impact:**
- Foreign aerospace companies face restrictions on procuring advanced Chinese manufacturing equipment
- Alternative suppliers (U.S., Europe, Japan) for single-crystal casting equipment face capacity constraints
- Research collaboration with Chinese aerospace institutions requires export license compliance
- Defense industry supply chains must verify Chinese-origin content in ballistic materials

**Compliance Timeline:**
- Approximately 30 days from announcement (May 30, 2024) to effective date (July 1, 2024)
- Existing contracts require retroactive permit applications
- Companies must review product portfolios against technical specifications to determine applicability
