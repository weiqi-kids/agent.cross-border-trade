## Summary

The US Department of Commerce fined Applied Materials (应用材料公司), a US semiconductor equipment maker, $252 million for illegally exporting ion implanters — core chip-manufacturing equipment — to China without the required export licenses. The equipment was first shipped to AMK in South Korea for assembly and then transshipped to China. The penalty is twice the value of the goods involved and is reportedly the second-highest fine ever issued by the Commerce Department's Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS).

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: US Department of Commerce / Bureau of Industry and Security (美国商务部工业和安全局, BIS) — announced 2026-02-11
- **Effective Date**: Penalty announced 2026-02-11
- **Scope**: Ion implanters (离子注入机) — core chip-manufacturing equipment; unlicensed exports/transshipment to China via South Korea

## Key Provisions

- Applied Materials shipped ion implanters to AMK (a South Korean company) for assembly, then transshipped them to China, without applying for or obtaining the required export licenses.
- Value of goods illegally shipped to China: approximately $126 million.
- Penalty: $252 million — twice the value of the goods involved; reportedly the second-highest fine ever issued by the US Commerce Department's BIS.
- The US Department of Justice and the Securities and Exchange Commission terminated related investigations without further action.
- Earlier (2023), the company had reportedly been under criminal investigation for evading export restrictions tied to a Chinese wafer foundry.
