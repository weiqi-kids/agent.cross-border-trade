## Summary

The US State Department's Defense Trade Controls Compliance office (under the Bureau of Political-Military Affairs) reached a $36 million civil-penalty settlement with General Electric Aerospace (通用电气航空航天公司, GE Aerospace) over 116 violations of the Arms Export Control Act (AECA) and International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR). Violations included unauthorized export of controlled technical data to China and other countries. The 36-month settlement requires compliance audits and additional remedial measures.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: US Department of State — Defense Trade Controls Compliance office, Bureau of Political-Military Affairs (美国国务院政治军事局国防贸易管制合规办公室)
- **Effective Date**: Settlement announced 2026-04-18; 36-month consent agreement period
- **Scope**: Defense articles and controlled technical data under AECA / ITAR; exports to China and other countries

## Key Provisions

- 116 violations, including:
  - Unauthorized export of controlled technical data to China and other countries (未经授权向中国等多个国家出口受控技术数据).
  - Breaching terms and conditions of licenses issued by the Defense Trade Controls office.
  - Unauthorized export of defense items to two countries.
  - Failure to report significant changes in ITAR registration information.
- Penalty: $36 million in civil penalties.
- 36-month settlement agreement.
- Requires at least one external audit of ITAR compliance programs.
- Mandates implementation of additional compliance measures.
- Underscores that defense exports must strictly follow proper State Department authorization.
