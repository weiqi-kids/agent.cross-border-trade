# 商务部公告2026年第11号 公布将20家日本实体列入出口管制管控名单（MOFCOM Announcement No. 11 of 2026: Addition of 20 Japanese Entities to Export Control List）

## Summary

China's Ministry of Commerce (MOFCOM) issued Announcement No. 11 of 2026 on February 24, 2026, adding 20 Japanese entities to the Export Control List (出口管制管控名单, "controlled entity list"). The listed entities span Japan's defense, aerospace, shipbuilding, electronics, and government research sectors, and were designated on the grounds that their activities enhance Japan's military capabilities, threatening Chinese national security interests and China's international non-proliferation obligations. The announcement took immediate effect upon publication and prohibits Chinese exporters from supplying dual-use items to these entities, and prohibits overseas parties from re-transferring Chinese-origin dual-use items to them. This is the largest single-event addition of Japanese entities to China's export control list and marks a significant escalation in China-Japan export control tensions.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce of the People's Republic of China, Bureau of Safety and Security Control (中华人民共和国商务部安全与管制局)
- **Effective Date**: 2026-02-24 (effective immediately upon publication)
- **Scope**: 20 Japanese entities across defense manufacturing, aerospace, shipbuilding, electronics, and government research institutions
- **Legal Basis**:
  - Export Control Law of the People's Republic of China (《中华人民共和国出口管制法》)
  - Regulations on Export Control of Dual-Use Items (《中华人民共和国两用物项出口管制条例》)
- **Stated Rationale**: To safeguard national security and interests; to fulfill international non-proliferation obligations; to counter Japan's "re-militarization" (再军事化) and alleged nuclear ambitions (拥核企图)

## Key Provisions

1. **Export Prohibition**: Chinese export operators are prohibited from exporting dual-use items (两用物项) to any of the 20 listed entities
2. **Re-transfer Ban**: Overseas organizations and individuals are prohibited from transferring or providing Chinese-origin dual-use items to the listed entities
3. **Immediate Cessation of Ongoing Activities**: Any existing contracts or activities involving the supply of dual-use items to listed entities must cease immediately
4. **Special Licensing Exception**: In exceptional circumstances where export is genuinely necessary, Chinese exporters may apply to MOFCOM for individual case-by-case approval

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

**Note: The following is a speculative analytical assessment based on the stated policy content and publicly available industry knowledge. It does not constitute confirmed fact.**

### 1. Immediate Operational Impact on Listed Entities

Chinese suppliers to any of the 20 entities must immediately suspend dual-use item deliveries. Given the breadth of the list — covering nearly all of Japan's major defense-industrial prime contractors — the disruption to Japanese defense procurement pipelines could be significant. Chinese-origin raw materials, specialty metals, precision components, and electronics commonly used in aerospace and naval programs are the most likely categories affected.

### 2. Mitsubishi Heavy Industries Group (5 Entities Listed)

Five MHI subsidiaries are listed, making MHI the most heavily targeted corporate group. MHI is Japan's largest defense contractor and a key producer of fighter aircraft components (F-2, F-35 supply chain), submarines, and naval vessels. Chinese-sourced specialty alloys, rare earth-containing components (e.g., for jet engine magnets), and precision machinery used in shipbuilding and aerospace manufacturing may now require alternative sourcing. Supply chain diversification timelines for MHI are likely to be measured in years, not months.

### 3. IHI Corporation (6 Entities Listed)

Six IHI subsidiaries are listed, covering jet engine manufacturing, aerospace structures, and power systems. IHI produces jet engines for Japan's military aircraft and components for rocket launch vehicles. The listing of IHI Aerospace and IHI Aerospace Engineering also threatens Japan's space launch capabilities if any Chinese-origin materials are involved in rocket propulsion or structural components.

### 4. Naval Shipbuilding Sector

Japan Marine United (JMU) and JMU Defense Systems are key builders of Japan Maritime Self-Defense Force (JMSDF) destroyers and submarines. MHI Shipbuilding and MHI Marine Machinery are also listed. This sector relies heavily on specialty steel, precision hydraulics, and electronics that may include Chinese-origin components. The combined listing of these shipbuilders suggests China is specifically targeting Japan's naval expansion program.

### 5. Defense Electronics and C4ISR

The inclusion of Fujitsu Defense & National Security, NEC Network and Sensor Systems, and NEC Aerospace Systems targets Japan's command, control, communications, computers, intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance (C4ISR) industrial base. This may affect radar systems, military communication platforms, and satellite ground systems. Chinese-origin electronic components and rare earth materials used in sensors could be restricted.

### 6. Government Research Institutions

The listing of JAXA (Japan's national space agency) and the National Defense Academy signals China's intent to restrict dual-use technology flows even to civilian-military hybrid research bodies. This is particularly significant: JAXA's inclusion suggests China views Japanese space programs as having military applications. This may complicate any remaining areas of space cooperation between China and Japan.

### 7. Broader Geopolitical and Trade Implications

- This announcement is paired with MOFCOM Announcement No. 12 of 2026, which established a Watch List (警告名单) for an additional 20 Japanese entities — indicating further escalation is anticipated
- The action appears to be a direct counter-response to Japan's expanding defense budget (reaching 2% of GDP by 2027), Japan's strengthened export controls on semiconductors and advanced materials targeting China, and Japan's closer defense alignment with the United States
- Chinese officials framed the action as targeted and limited to dual-use items, explicitly stating it does not affect "normal China-Japan trade" — but the listing of 20 major entities will create significant compliance complexity for Chinese exporters across multiple sectors
- Japanese entities not yet listed but in the broader defense ecosystem (e.g., Toshiba Infrastructure Systems, Honda Aero, Subaru Aerospace) may face increased scrutiny and pre-emptive supply chain adjustments

### 8. Compliance Burden on Chinese Exporters

All Chinese companies exporting dual-use items to Japan must now conduct enhanced end-user screening against this list before proceeding with any transaction. Given the scale and reputational significance of the listed entities, the compliance burden across China's export community — particularly in metals, electronics, chemicals, and precision machinery — will be substantial.
