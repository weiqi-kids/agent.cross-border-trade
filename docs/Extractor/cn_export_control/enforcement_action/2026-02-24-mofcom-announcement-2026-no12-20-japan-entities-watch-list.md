## Summary

On February 24, 2026, China's Ministry of Commerce (MOFCOM) issued Announcement 2026 No. 12, designating 20 Japanese entities to its Concern List (關注名單 / 关注名单) under the authority of the Export Control Law of the People's Republic of China and the Regulations on Export Control of Dual-Use Items. The stated basis for designation is the inability to verify the final end-users and end-uses of dual-use items exported to these entities. Effective immediately upon publication, exporters must apply for single-item licenses for each transaction with listed entities, submit risk assessments, and provide written commitments that items will not be used to enhance Japan's military capabilities; standard statutory review timelines are suspended for these applications.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce of the People's Republic of China (MOFCOM), Safety and Control Bureau (安全与管制局)
- **Document Number**: 商务部公告2026年第12号 (MOFCOM Announcement 2026 No. 12)
- **Effective Date**: 2026-02-24 (upon publication — 本公告自公布之日起正式实施)
- **Scope**: 20 Japanese entities across aerospace, automotive, electronics, chemicals, materials, and academic sectors; applies to all dual-use item (两用物项) exports from China to these entities
- **Legal Basis**: 《中华人民共和国出口管制法》(Export Control Law of the PRC) and 《中华人民共和国两用物项出口管制条例》(Regulations on Export Control of Dual-Use Items of the PRC)

## Key Provisions

1. **License Restriction**: Exporters selling dual-use items to any of the 20 listed entities may not use general licenses or registration-based export permits. A single-item (单项) license is required for each transaction.

2. **Risk Assessment Requirement**: License applications must include a risk assessment report specifically addressing the risks of exporting to a Concern List entity.

3. **Written Commitment**: Applicants must provide a written commitment (书面承诺) that the dual-use items will not be used for any purpose that enhances Japan's military capabilities (有助于提升日本军事实力用途).

4. **Suspended Review Deadlines**: Standard statutory license review timelines under Article 17, Paragraph 1 of the Dual-Use Items Export Control Regulations are suspended for applications involving these entities.

5. **Heightened End-User/End-Use Scrutiny**: MOFCOM will apply stricter final end-user and end-use review for all dual-use item exports to listed entities. Exports involving Japanese military end-users, military end-uses, or any use that could enhance Japan's military capabilities will not be approved.

6. **Removal Mechanism**: Listed entities may apply for delisting by fulfilling their verification cooperation obligations under Article 26 of the Dual-Use Items Export Control Regulations. MOFCOM retains discretion to remove an entity after verification.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

> **Disclaimer**: The following is a speculative analytical assessment based on the publicly available policy text. It does not constitute legal advice. Businesses affected by this designation should seek qualified legal counsel.

### Immediate Operational Impact

**Exporters (Chinese side)**: Any Chinese exporter currently supplying dual-use items to these 20 entities faces immediate compliance obligations. General licenses are invalidated for these counterparties. Each shipment now requires a new single-item license application, with no guaranteed timeline for approval. This effectively creates an indefinite hold on new dual-use export transactions to the listed entities.

**Importers (Japanese side)**: The 20 designated entities — spanning aerospace (Subaru, FUJI Aerospace, Mitsui Bussan Aerospace), advanced materials (Mitsubishi Materials, Nitto Denko, NOF Corporation), heavy industry (Sumitomo Heavy Industries, Hino Motors), and electronics (TDK, Tokin, Nissin Electric) — may face sourcing disruptions for:
- Specialty chemicals and reagents (NOF, Nacalai Tesque)
- Advanced materials and composites (Mitsubishi Materials, Nitto Denko)
- Precision electronic components (TDK, Tokin)
- Industrial equipment and machinery (Sumitomo Heavy Industries, Yashima Denki)

### Geopolitical Context

This action is the largest single Concern List designation targeting Japanese entities since China established the dual-use item control framework. It follows Japan's 2023 alignment with US-led semiconductor export controls, Japan's defense spending increases to 2% of GDP, and the trilateral Japan-US-South Korea security coordination. The designation of an academic institution (Institute of Science Tokyo) signals China's intent to scrutinize technology transfer pathways beyond purely commercial channels.

The explicit restriction phrase "有助于提升日本军事实力" (any use that enhances Japan's military capabilities) mirrors language used in earlier China Entity List-style actions and represents a significant escalation in China's use of export control as a geopolitical instrument directed at a G7 partner.

### Third-Country Supply Chain Implications

- **Substitution pressure**: Listed entities will likely accelerate procurement diversification away from Chinese-origin dual-use items, benefiting suppliers in the US, EU, South Korea, and Taiwan.
- **Re-export risk**: Third-country intermediaries that procure Chinese dual-use items for onward supply to listed Japanese entities are also at risk; MOFCOM's end-use review will scrutinize the full supply chain.
- **Contract force majeure**: Existing contracts between Chinese exporters and listed entities may trigger force majeure or material adverse change clauses pending license outcomes.
