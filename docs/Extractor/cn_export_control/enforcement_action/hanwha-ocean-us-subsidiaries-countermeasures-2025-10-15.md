## Summary

China's Ministry of Commerce (MOFCOM) issued Order No.6 of 2025, signed by Minister Wang Wentao, imposing counter-sanctions on five US-related subsidiaries of South Korea's Hanwha Ocean Corporation (韩华海洋株式会社). The measures respond to the US Section 301 investigation into China's maritime, logistics and shipbuilding industries, which China deems a serious violation of international law. Organizations and individuals within China are prohibited from engaging in transactions or cooperation with the five listed entities.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce of the People's Republic of China (中华人民共和国商务部); approved under the National Coordination Mechanism for Counter-Foreign Sanctions Work
- **Effective Date**: 2025-10-14
- **Scope**: Shipbuilding, maritime and shipping logistics sector; targets five US-incorporated subsidiaries of Hanwha Ocean; binds all Chinese-domestic organizations and individuals

## Key Provisions

- **Legal basis**: 《中华人民共和国反外国制裁法》(Anti-Foreign Sanctions Law of the PRC), Articles 3, 4, 6, 9, 10, 15, and its implementing regulations Articles 3, 5, 8, 10.
- **Five listed entities**:
  1. Hanwha Shipping LLC
  2. Hanwha Philly Shipyard Inc.
  3. Hanwha Ocean USA International LLC
  4. Hanwha Shipping Holdings LLC
  5. HS USA Holdings Corp.
- **Countermeasure**: Organizations and individuals within China are prohibited from engaging in relevant transactions, cooperation and other activities with the five listed entities (禁止中国境内的组织和个人与其进行有关交易、合作等活动).
- **Trigger**: US Section 301 investigation and measures against China's maritime, logistics and shipbuilding industries.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*The following is speculative analysis.* The countermeasures cut the five Hanwha entities off from Chinese counterparties, potentially affecting Hanwha Ocean's US shipbuilding operations (including Philly Shipyard) that may rely on Chinese-sourced components, equipment or financing. This signals an escalation of the US-China maritime/shipbuilding dispute into the corporate-sanctions domain, raising compliance risk for global shipbuilding and shipping supply chains with exposure to both Chinese suppliers and US-linked Hanwha entities.
