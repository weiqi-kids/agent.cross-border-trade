## Summary

MOFCOM announced adjustments to export control measures for US entities following the China-US Kuala Lumpur economic and trade consultation. Starting November 10, 2025, China will suspend export control measures for 15 US entities from the March 4 announcement, and continue to suspend measures for 16 US entities from the April 4 announcement for one year. Exporters wishing to export dual-use items to these entities must apply to MOFCOM under the Dual-Use Items Export Control Regulation.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce of PRC (MOFCOM)
- **Effective Date**: 2025-11-10
- **Scope**: 31 US entities previously on the Export Control List

## Key Provisions

### Background
- March 4, 2025 (Announcement No. 13): 15 US entities added to export control list
- April 4, 2025 (Announcement No. 21): 16 US entities added to export control list
- Total: 31 US entities prohibited from receiving dual-use items

### Adjustment Measures (Effective November 10, 2025)
1. **15 entities from March 4 announcement**: Measures **suspended**
2. **16 entities from April 4 announcement**: Measures **continued suspension for 1 year**

### Export Procedure
- Exporters must apply to MOFCOM for dual-use item exports to these entities
- Applications reviewed under the Dual-Use Items Export Control Regulation
- Compliant applications will be approved
