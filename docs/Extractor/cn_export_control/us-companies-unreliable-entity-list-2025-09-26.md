## Summary
China's Ministry of Commerce sanctioned three American companies—Saronic Technology, Alcon, and International Maritime Engineering—for conducting military technology cooperation with Taiwan. The action invokes foreign trade law, national security law, and anti-foreign sanctions legislation, emphasizing that sanctions apply only to entities endangering national security while law-abiding foreign companies have no reason for concern.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce (商务部)
- **Effective Date**: 2025-09-26
- **Scope**: Three U.S. companies: Saronic Technology, Alcon, and International Maritime Engineering

## Key Provisions
- **Legal Basis**: Foreign Trade Law, National Security Law, and Anti-Foreign Sanctions Law
- **Grounds for Listing**: Companies conducted military technology cooperation with Taiwan despite strong opposition
- **Impact**: Companies caused damage to China's sovereignty and security interests
- **Scope of Application**: Unreliable entity list applies only to entities endangering national security
- **Reassurance**: Law-abiding foreign companies have no reason for concern
- **Commitment**: China maintains commitment to welcoming legitimate foreign investment and providing stable business conditions

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment
This enforcement action specifically targets U.S. defense contractors engaged in Taiwan-related military cooperation, a core red line for China. The unreliable entity list designation likely restricts these companies' ability to conduct business in China, access Chinese markets, or partner with Chinese entities. The explicit naming of companies (unlike the previous dual-use export control list) signals strong political messaging. Supply chain implications are sector-specific, primarily affecting defense and maritime technology sectors with Taiwan exposure.
