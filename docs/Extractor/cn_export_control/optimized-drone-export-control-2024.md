# 优化调整无人机出口管制措施 (Optimized Drone Export Control Measures)

## Summary

Three government departments issued Announcement No. 31 of 2024, implementing optimized drone export control measures effective September 1, 2024. The announcement establishes controls on four categories: aerospace engines exceeding 16kW designed for unmanned aircraft, advanced payload systems, long-range radio communication equipment, and counter-UAS systems.

---

## Key Details

**Issuing Authority:**
- Ministry of Commerce (商务部)
- General Administration of Customs (海关总署)
- Central Military Commission Equipment Development Department (中央军委装备发展部)

**Announcement Number:** 2024 No. 31

**Publication Date:** 2024-07-31

**Effective Date:** 2024-09-01

## Key Provisions

### 1. Aerospace Engines (航空发动机)

**Technical Specifications:**
- Power output exceeding 16 kW
- Specifically designed for unmanned aircraft
- Multiple HS codes referenced: 8501200010, 8407101010, 8411111010, and others

### 2. Payload Systems (载荷系统)

**Infrared Imaging Devices:**
- Operating wavelengths: 780-30,000 nm
- Instantaneous Field of View (IFOV) under 2.5 mrad

**Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR):**
- Detection range: 5 km or greater

**Laser Designators:**
- Operating temperature: functional above 55°C

**Inertial Measurement Equipment:**
- Meeting specified precision standards for navigation

### 3. Radio Communication Equipment (无线电通信设备)

**Controlled Parameters:**
- Transmission distance exceeding 50 kilometers
- Capability to control multiple aircraft simultaneously

### 4. Counter-UAS Systems (反无人机系统)

**Electronic Jamming Equipment:**
- Effective range: 5 km or greater

**High-Power Lasers:**
- Output power exceeding 1.5 kW

### 5. Export Licensing Procedures

**Application Process:**
- Submit through provincial commerce departments to Ministry of Commerce
- Required documentation:
  - Export contracts
  - Technical specifications
  - End-user certifications
  - Identity documentation

**Enforcement:**
"违反本公告规定的,依据有关法律法规承担相应的行政责任或刑事责任" — Violations incur administrative penalties or criminal prosecution per export control law provisions.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

**Impact Level:** Very High

**Affected Industries:**
- Commercial drone manufacturing (DJI, Autel, etc.)
- Industrial UAV systems for agriculture, surveying, inspection
- Defense and security drone systems
- Drone component suppliers (engines, sensors, communication systems)
- Counter-drone technology manufacturers

**Geographic Considerations (speculative):**
- China dominates global commercial drone manufacturing (~70% market share)
- Major export markets: North America, Europe, Middle East, Southeast Asia
- Military and dual-use drone exports to Middle East and African countries may face enhanced scrutiny
- Commercial agricultural drone exports less likely to trigger controls (typically lower power engines)

**Technical Threshold Analysis (speculative):**
- 16 kW engine threshold targets medium-to-large UAVs (small consumer drones typically <5 kW)
- 50 km communication range threshold captures tactical and long-endurance systems
- Infrared IFOV specification targets high-resolution surveillance payloads
- Counter-UAS controls prevent proliferation of anti-drone technology

**Compliance Requirements:**
- Manufacturers must classify drone systems and components against technical thresholds
- Bundled systems (complete drones) require analysis of individual components
- End-user verification particularly critical for government and military customers
- Technology transfer and training services may also require licenses

## Notes

The announcement uses the term "optimized" (优化调整), suggesting this represents a refinement of previous drone export controls rather than entirely new measures. The specific technical thresholds indicate an intent to balance commercial export interests with proliferation concerns.

The inclusion of the Central Military Commission Equipment Development Department as a joint issuing authority emphasizes the military significance of these controls. The three-party coordination mechanism (MOFCOM, Customs, CMC) will likely require enhanced interagency consultation for licensing decisions.

China's dominant position in global drone manufacturing gives these controls significant impact on international markets. The timing may reflect concerns about proliferation of advanced UAV capabilities to conflict zones and non-state actors.
