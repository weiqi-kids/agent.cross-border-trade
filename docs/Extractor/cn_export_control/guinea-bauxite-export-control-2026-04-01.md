## Summary

Guinea, the world's largest bauxite producer, is in negotiations with mining companies to restrict the volume of ore placed on the market in order to halt falling commodity prices. The policy mirrors export control measures recently enacted by neighboring African nations including the Democratic Republic of Congo (cobalt) and Zimbabwe (lithium ore). Controls are expected to take effect within weeks, targeting alignment of 2026 production with companies' feasibility study volumes.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Guinea Ministry of Mines (Minister Bouna Sylla)
- **Effective Date**: Within weeks of April 1, 2026 (exact date not specified)
- **Scope**: Bauxite (铝土矿) exports from Guinea; affects global alumina and aluminum supply chains

## Key Provisions

- Guinea will negotiate production quotas with mining companies to "control the quantity of ore placed on the market"
- Policy objective: regulate bauxite production to prevent further price declines and protect government tax revenues and corporate revenues
- Mining enterprises directed to respect production limits specified in their licenses and contracts; some companies reportedly have been exceeding authorized capacity
- 2026 production targets to align with feasibility study projections
- Guinea also pursuing a longer-term strategy to refine bauxite domestically into alumina rather than exporting raw ore, increasing domestic value-add

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

> *The following is an analytical assessment, not confirmed fact.*

- **Aluminum supply chain**: Guinea exported over 183 million tons of bauxite in 2025 (a 25% year-on-year increase); production quotas could meaningfully tighten global alumina and aluminum supply
- **Price impact**: The policy is designed to arrest price declines; successful implementation would likely cause bauxite and alumina prices to stabilize or increase
- **China exposure**: China is the world's largest importer of bauxite from Guinea; Chinese aluminum smelters and manufacturers face potential raw material supply risk if quotas are strictly enforced
- **Alternative suppliers**: Australia and Brazil can partially offset Guinean supply reductions; however, substitution is not immediate
- **African export control trend**: The Congo-Zimbabwe-Guinea pattern signals a broader African mineral producer trend toward export controls on critical minerals—watch for further restrictions on lithium, cobalt, manganese, and other battery metals
- **Downstream impact**: Global electric vehicle and battery manufacturers dependent on African critical minerals face increased procurement complexity
