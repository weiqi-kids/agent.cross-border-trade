## Summary

China's Ministry of Commerce issued Announcement No. 12 of 2026, adding 20 Japanese entities whose end-users and end-uses for dual-use items cannot be verified to the Watch List (关注名单). Unlike the Control List (Announcement No. 11), entities on the Watch List face enhanced licensing requirements rather than outright export bans. Exporters must submit risk assessment reports and written commitments that dual-use items will not be used to enhance Japan's military capabilities. Effective immediately from 2026-02-24.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce, Bureau of Safety and Security Control (安全与管制局)
- **Effective Date**: 2026-02-24 (effective upon publication)
- **Scope**: 20 Japanese entities across automotive, materials, electronics, and research sectors
- **Legal Basis**: Export Control Law of the PRC and Regulations on Export Control of Dual-Use Items

## Key Provisions

1. **General License Prohibition**: Exporters cannot use general licenses or registration-based export permits for dual-use items to listed entities
2. **Individual License Required**: Must apply for individual export licenses with:
   - Risk assessment report on the Watch List entity
   - Written commitment that dual-use items will not be used for any purpose enhancing Japan's military capabilities
3. **Extended Review Period**: License review periods are not subject to the normal time limits under Article 17(1) of the Dual-Use Items Export Control Regulations
4. **Stricter End-User/End-Use Verification**: MOFCOM will conduct more rigorous end-user and end-use reviews
5. **Military Use Denial**: Exports involving Japanese military users, military purposes, or any purpose enhancing Japan's military capabilities will not be approved
6. **Removal Mechanism**: Listed entities may apply for removal by cooperating with verification obligations under Article 26 of the Regulations
