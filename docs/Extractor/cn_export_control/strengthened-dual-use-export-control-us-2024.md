# 加强相关两用物项对美国出口管制 (Strengthened Export Controls on Dual-Use Items to the United States)

**Source:** 商务部 (Ministry of Commerce)

**Date:** 2024-12-17
**Category:** `policy_guidance`

**Confidence:** High

## Summary

The Ministry of Commerce issued Announcement No. 46 of 2024, implementing strengthened export controls on dual-use items to the United States, effective December 3, 2024. The announcement establishes comprehensive prohibitions on exports to US military users and generally denies licenses for gallium, germanium, antimony, and superhard materials exports to the US, with enhanced verification procedures for graphite-related items.

---

## Key Details

**Issuing Authority:** Ministry of Commerce (商务部)

**Effective Date:** 2024-12-03 (upon publication)

**Scope:** Dual-use items with focus on five critical material categories

---

## Key Provisions

### 1. Prohibition on Military End-Use/End-User

"禁止向美国军事用户或用于军事目的出口两用物项" — Prohibition on exporting dual-use items to US military users or for military purposes

### 2. General License Denial for Strategic Materials

The following materials face general denial of export licenses to the United States:
- **Gallium (镓)** — Critical for compound semiconductors and power electronics
- **Germanium (锗)** — Essential for infrared optics and fiber optics
- **Antimony (锑)** — Used in flame retardants and military applications
- **Superhard Materials (超硬材料)** — Including synthetic diamond for cutting tools

### 3. Enhanced End-User Verification for Graphite

"对石墨等物项出口实施更加严格的最终用户和最终用途审查" — Graphite-related items face enhanced end-user and end-use verification procedures

### 4. Enforcement Provisions

"对于违反本公告规定,将中国原产两用物项转售给美国相关实体的组织和个人,依据有关规定追究相应的法律责任" — Organizations and individuals violating these provisions by redirecting Chinese-origin dual-use items to US entities face legal consequences

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

**Impact Level:** Very High

**Affected Industries:**
- Semiconductor and power electronics (gallium compounds)
- Infrared and optical systems (germanium)
- Defense and flame retardant applications (antimony)
- Precision machining and cutting tools (superhard materials)
- Battery and energy storage systems (graphite)

**Geographic Considerations (speculative):**
- US-based manufacturers dependent on Chinese supplies of these materials face immediate procurement challenges
- Third-country entities may face scrutiny if suspected of serving as transshipment points to US end-users
- Alternative suppliers (Canada for graphite, France for germanium) have limited capacity to replace Chinese supply volumes
- US domestic production initiatives for these materials will likely accelerate but require years to reach commercial scale

**Strategic Implications (speculative):**
- Timing suggests response to US semiconductor export control measures
- General license denial policy represents stricter enforcement than case-by-case review
- Enhanced graphite controls target battery supply chains and defense applications
- Military end-user prohibition extends beyond specific materials to all dual-use items

**Compliance Requirements:**
- Exporters must verify end-user and end-use declarations for all US-bound dual-use items
- Applications for gallium, germanium, antimony, and superhard materials exports to the US should anticipate denials
- Enhanced documentation required for graphite exports, including detailed end-user certifications
- Third-party distribution channels must implement controls to prevent unauthorized re-export to US entities

---

## Notes

This announcement represents China's most significant country-specific export control measure, establishing a de facto embargo on key strategic materials to the United States. The "general denial" policy language indicates that export licenses for the specified materials will be routinely denied absent exceptional circumstances. The enforcement provision targeting transshipment activities demonstrates intent to prevent circumvention through third countries.
