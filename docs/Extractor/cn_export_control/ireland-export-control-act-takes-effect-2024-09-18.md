## Summary
Ireland's Minister for Trade Promotion, Digital and Company Regulation announced on August 22, 2024, that the 2023 Export Control Act officially took effect, replacing the 2008 Export Control Act. The new law modernizes Ireland's export control framework and introduces a new Export Authorization System (EAS) to streamline application procedures.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: Irish Department of Enterprise, Trade and Employment
- **Effective Date**: 2024-08-22
- **Scope**: Replaces 2008 Export Control Act; applies to all exports subject to Irish export control regulations

## Key Provisions
- **Legislative Update**: The 2023 Export Control Act replaces the 2008 Act and associated regulations
- **Modernization Objective**: Strengthens Ireland's ability to meet EU and international export control obligations
- **New IT System**: Launch of Export Authorization System (EAS) on August 22, 2024
- **Streamlined Procedures**: EAS designed to simplify and digitize export license application processes
- **International Alignment**: Ensures compliance with European Union and international export control commitments
