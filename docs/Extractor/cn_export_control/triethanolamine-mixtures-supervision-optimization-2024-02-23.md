## Summary
The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, Ministry of Commerce, and General Administration of Customs jointly announced regulatory streamlining for low-concentration triethanolamine mixtures in consumer goods. Effective February 1, 2024, products including non-medical disinfectants, synthetic detergent powders, cosmetics, and inks no longer require import/export licensing or monitored chemical approval procedures under China's Chemical Weapons Convention compliance framework.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, Ministry of Commerce, General Administration of Customs (three-agency joint announcement)
- **Effective Date**: 2024-02-01
- **Scope**: Consumer products containing low-concentration triethanolamine including non-medical disinfectants, synthetic detergent powders, cosmetics, and inks

## Key Provisions

**Regulatory Exemptions:**
- Products no longer require import/export licensing procedures
- No monitored chemical approval required
- Excluded from Dual-Use Items and Technology Import/Export License Management Catalog

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- Low-concentration triethanolamine in consumer goods
- Minimal proliferation risks
- Specified product categories detailed in attached product list (PDF)

**Covered Product Categories:**
1. Non-medical disinfectants
2. Synthetic detergent powders
3. Cosmetics
4. Inks

**Implementation Mechanism:**
- Detailed product list specifies qualifying low-concentration triethanolamine mixtures
- Agencies instructed to report implementation issues for future regulatory adjustments

**Legal Framework:**
- Aligned with China's Chemical Weapons Convention compliance obligations
- Part of monitored chemicals control system optimization
