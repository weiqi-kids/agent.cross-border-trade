# 国家禁毒委员会办公室发布通告 提醒相关企业和个人防范制毒物品和非列管可制毒化学品及设备流失（National Narcotics Control Commission Office Notice on Preventing Diversion of Drug Precursor Substances, Non-Listed Precursor Chemicals, and Equipment）

## Summary

The National Narcotics Control Commission (NNCC) Office issued a notice reminding enterprises and individuals involved in the production, sale, transport, or import/export of drug precursor substances (制毒物品) and non-listed precursor chemicals (非列管可制毒化学品) of their legal obligations and compliance requirements. The notice outlines 13 key provisions covering prohibitions on illegal activities, shared criminal liability, customs compliance, online platform obligations, and due diligence requirements for export transactions. It specifically highlights monitoring obligations for logistics, postal, and freight-forwarding companies, and draws attention to relevant laws in destination countries including the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: National Narcotics Control Commission (NNCC) Office (国家禁毒委员会办公室); disseminated via Ministry of Commerce Export Control Information Network (安全与管制局)
- **Effective Date**: Immediate upon publication (2025-11-11)
- **Scope**: All organizations and individuals engaged in the production, sale, transport, import, or export of drug precursor chemicals (listed and non-listed) and dual-use equipment with potential drug manufacturing applications

## Key Provisions

1. **Prohibited Activities**: Bans on illegal production, sale, transport, or cross-border movement of listed precursor chemicals such as acetic anhydride (乙酸酐) and ether (乙醚) used in drug manufacturing.

2. **Shared Criminal Liability**: Persons knowingly providing non-listed precursor chemicals or equipment for illegal drug manufacturing face prosecution as accomplices (共犯) under criminal law.

3. **Tax Compliance**: Enterprises must fulfill tax obligations; tax evasion in connection with precursor chemical transactions carries administrative penalties and potential criminal charges.

4. **Customs Law Compliance**: Smuggling precursor chemicals or misrepresenting cargo manifests constitutes a customs violation, resulting in seizure of goods and possible criminal prosecution.

5. **Online Platform Obligations**: Network operators and online marketplace platforms must monitor user-generated content, remove prohibited information, and report suspected violations to authorities.

6. **Financial and Foreign Exchange Compliance**: Entities must adhere to anti-money laundering (AML) regulations and foreign exchange management requirements.

7. **Due Diligence for Export Transactions**: Companies are required to verify buyer credentials and end-use, scrutinize unusual or suspicious overseas orders, and proactively report suspected violations.

8. **Internet Sales Restrictions**: Only licensed entities may advertise non-pharmaceutical precursor chemicals online; sales or advertising by non-licensed individuals are prohibited.

9. **Monitoring of Destination-Country Laws**: Exporters must monitor and comply with relevant laws in destination countries, with specific reference to regulations in the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

10. **Postal and Courier Obligations**: Postal and courier companies must implement internal controls, conduct suspicious-shipment screening, and report suspected precursor chemical diversion.

11. **Logistics Company Obligations**: Freight and logistics companies must maintain shipment records, conduct customer due diligence, and report suspicious activity.

12. **Freight Forwarding Obligations**: Freight forwarding agents must not knowingly facilitate the transport of precursor chemicals for illegal purposes and must maintain compliance records.

13. **General Reporting Duty**: All covered entities have an affirmative duty to report suspected diversion of precursor chemicals or equipment to the appropriate authorities.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*Note: The following represents a speculative analytical assessment and does not constitute legal advice.*

- **Chemical manufacturers and traders**: Companies producing or trading acetic anhydride, ether, gamma-butyrolactone (GBL), and other dual-use chemicals face heightened compliance scrutiny. Know-Your-Customer (KYC) processes for export orders—particularly to the Americas—should be reviewed.
- **E-commerce and online platforms**: Platforms hosting chemical product listings must implement enhanced content moderation and user verification, consistent with existing obligations under China's E-Commerce Law.
- **Logistics and freight-forwarding sector**: The explicit inclusion of postal, courier, logistics, and freight-forwarding companies signals that supply chain intermediaries are now within the compliance perimeter for precursor chemical controls, not merely end-manufacturers.
- **Export-facing enterprises**: The reminder to monitor U.S., Canadian, and Mexican law creates an extraterritorial compliance dimension; Chinese exporters shipping to North America may need to align with DEA regulations and import controls at the destination.
- **Risk level**: Moderate for general chemical exporters; High for enterprises with existing exposure to precursor chemical categories.
