## Summary

A MOFCOM spokesperson stated that China firmly opposes the EU's listing of Chinese enterprises in its 20th package of sanctions against Russia, calling it disregard for China's repeated representations and objections. China rejects unilateral sanctions lacking UN Security Council authorization and EU "long-arm jurisdiction," demands the EU remove the Chinese entities and individuals from its lists, and warns it will take necessary measures to protect Chinese enterprises' legitimate rights.

## Key Provisions

- **Position**: China "坚决反对" (firmly opposes) the EU's "肆无忌惮列单中国企业" (reckless listing of Chinese enterprises) in the 20th round of Russia sanctions, despite repeated Chinese representations and objections.
- **Legal/principle objections**: China firmly opposes unilateral sanctions without UN Security Council authorization and rejects EU "long-arm jurisdiction" (长臂管辖) targeting Chinese entities and individuals.
- **Demand**: The EU should immediately remove Chinese companies and individuals from the sanctions lists and resolve differences through dialogue consistent with the consensus reached by the two sides' leaders.
- **Warning / countermeasures**: China "将采取必要措施" (will take necessary measures) to safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of Chinese enterprises, and all consequences will be borne by the EU.
