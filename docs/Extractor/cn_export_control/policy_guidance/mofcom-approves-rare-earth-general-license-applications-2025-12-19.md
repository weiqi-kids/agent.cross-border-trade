## Summary

A MOFCOM spokesperson confirmed that China has approved general license (通用许可 / general license) applications for certain rare earth exporters who have preliminarily met the basic requirements. Qualifying Chinese exporters with relevant compliance experience can now obtain general licenses rather than case-by-case individual licenses, representing a streamlining of the rare earth export control licensing regime.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: China Ministry of Commerce Spokesperson (商务部新闻发言人)
- **Effective Date**: N/A (spokesperson Q&A issued 2025-12-19; approvals ongoing)
- **Scope**: Rare earth materials and related items subject to export controls; qualifying Chinese rare earth exporters

## Key Provisions

- **Action**: MOFCOM has approved general license applications for some rare earth exporters, stating "部分中国出口商已初步达到申请通用许可的基本要求" (certain Chinese exporters have preliminarily met the basic requirements to apply for general licenses).
- **Mechanism**: Qualifying exporters move from individual case-by-case licensing to general licenses (longer-validity, broader-scope permits).
- **Eligibility conditions**: Applicants must demonstrate fulfillment of basic compliance requirements, practical experience with export procedures and regulations, and submission of a formal general license application.
- **Context**: This follows the implementation of China's rare earth export controls and allows qualifying domestic exporters streamlined access to international markets.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*The following is speculative analysis.*

Granting general licenses to qualifying rare earth exporters could ease bottlenecks created by case-by-case licensing, improving predictability and lead times for downstream buyers in magnets, EVs, electronics, and defense supply chains that depend on Chinese rare earths. The move may partially relieve supply uncertainty introduced by earlier rare earth export controls, though benefits are limited to exporters that meet the compliance thresholds.
