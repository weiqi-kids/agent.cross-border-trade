## Summary

A MOFCOM spokesperson responded to the US House Foreign Affairs Committee's passage (on April 22) of the MATCH Act and other export-control bills, stating that China consistently opposes the generalization of national security and the abuse of export controls. China warned the measures would severely disrupt international trade and economic order and threaten the stability of the global semiconductor supply chain, and said it will closely follow the legislative process and take necessary countermeasures.
