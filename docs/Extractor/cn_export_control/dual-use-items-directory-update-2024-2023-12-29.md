## Summary
The Ministry of Commerce and General Administration of Customs issued Announcement 2023 No. 66, updating the Dual-Use Items and Technology Import-Export License Management Directory for 2024. The new directory supersedes the 2022 version and takes effect January 1, 2024, maintaining controls on strategic dual-use items while updating radioactive material import procedures.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce and General Administration of Customs
- **Effective Date**: 2024-01-01
- **Scope**: All importers and exporters of dual-use items and technologies listed in the management directory; specific enhanced requirements for radioactive isotopes

## Key Provisions

**Legal Framework:**
- Implemented under Export Control Law of the People's Republic of China
- Administrative basis: 2005 State Council Regulation No. 29 on dual-use items licensing

**Radioactive Materials Special Provisions:**
- Importers handling radioactive isotopes must obtain approval from Ministry of Ecology and Environment
- Subsequent application for import permits through Commerce Ministry's quota licensing bureau required
- Import permits mandatory before customs clearance

**Implementation Timeline:**
- Updated directory applies to all import-export operations beginning January 1, 2024
- Previous 2022 directory (Announcement No. 42) rescinded effective January 1, 2024

**Documentation:**
- Complete management directory available as attached PDF document
- Directory details specific controlled items and technology categories

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

**Affected Industry Sectors:**
- Nuclear energy and medical isotope supply chains requiring radioactive material imports
- Advanced manufacturing sectors using dual-use technologies
- Research institutions importing controlled scientific equipment
- Defense contractors and aerospace companies with dual-use component needs

**Procedural Changes:**
- Additional approval layer from Ministry of Ecology and Environment for radioactive materials
- Extended lead times for import licensing processes
- Enhanced documentation requirements for end-use verification

**Compliance Requirements:**
- Companies must review 2024 directory against 2022 version to identify newly controlled items
- Update internal compliance programs to reflect new classification criteria
- Establish coordination protocols with multiple government agencies for radioactive material imports
- Train customs brokers and logistics partners on new directory requirements

**Trade Flow Implications:**
- Potential delays for shipments of newly controlled items during Q1 2024 adjustment period
- Increased administrative burden for companies with diverse dual-use item portfolios
- Need for advance planning to secure licenses before shipment deadlines
- Enhanced scrutiny on technology transfers and technical documentation

**Strategic Context:**
- Annual directory updates reflect evolving technology landscape and security priorities
- Aligns with international non-proliferation commitments while addressing national security concerns
- Radioactive material provisions indicate increased focus on nuclear security and environmental protection
