## Summary
China's Ministry of Commerce and General Administration of Customs issued Announcement 2025 No. 58, implementing export controls on lithium battery materials and artificial graphite negative electrode materials, effective November 8, 2025. The controls cover high-energy-density batteries, positive and negative electrode materials, production equipment, and manufacturing technologies critical to global electric vehicle and energy storage industries.

## Key Details
- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Commerce & General Administration of Customs (商务部 & 海关总署)
- **Effective Date**: November 8, 2025
- **Scope**: Complete lithium battery supply chain from raw materials to production equipment and technology

## Key Provisions

**Legal Basis:**
Based on national security and non-proliferation obligations under Chinese export control law.

**Controlled Items:**

**1. Lithium Battery Category:**

**High Energy Density Batteries:**
- Lithium-ion batteries with energy density ≥300 Wh/kg
- Used in electric vehicles, aerospace, and advanced applications

**Manufacturing Equipment:**
- Winding machines (for cylindrical cells)
- Stacking machines (for prismatic/pouch cells)
- Electrolyte injection systems
- Formation and testing equipment

**Production Technology:**
- Cell design and manufacturing processes
- Battery management system (BMS) technology
- Safety and thermal management technologies

**2. Positive Electrode (Cathode) Materials:**

**Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP):**
- Specific density ≥2.5 g/cm³ (high-performance grade)
- Dominant chemistry for commercial EVs and energy storage

**Ternary Cathode Precursor Materials:**
- NCM (Nickel-Cobalt-Manganese) precursors
- NCA (Nickel-Cobalt-Aluminum) precursors
- Used in high-energy-density batteries

**Rich-Lithium Manganese-Based Materials:**
- Next-generation high-capacity cathode materials
- Emerging technology for advanced batteries

**Production Equipment:**
- Calcination furnaces (high-temperature processing)
- Mixers and blenders
- Mills and pulverizers (particle size control)

**3. Negative Electrode (Anode) Materials:**

**Artificial Graphite:**
- Synthetic graphite negative electrode materials
- Industry standard for high-performance lithium-ion batteries

**Natural/Artificial Graphite Composites:**
- Mixed natural and artificial graphite materials
- Optimized for specific performance characteristics

**Production Equipment:**
- Granulation equipment (particle forming)
- Graphitization furnaces (high-temperature treatment at 2500-3000°C)
- Coating equipment (surface modification for performance enhancement)

**Production Technologies:**
- Graphitization process know-how
- Coating formulations and processes
- Quality control methodologies

**Compliance Requirements:**

Exporters must:
- Obtain approval from the Commerce Department
- Accurately identify controlled items on customs declarations
- Note two-use item export control codes
- Disclose technical parameters for uncontrolled items with similar specifications

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

**Global Market Context:**

China's dominance in lithium battery supply chain:
- **Battery Manufacturing**: ~75% of global lithium-ion battery production
- **Cathode Materials**: ~70% of global LFP production, ~60% of ternary materials
- **Anode Materials**: ~90%+ of artificial graphite production
- **Production Equipment**: ~60-70% of battery manufacturing equipment

**Critical Chokepoints:**

1. **Artificial Graphite Anode**:
   - China controls ~95% of global production
   - Graphitization process requires specialized high-temperature furnaces (capital intensive, energy intensive)
   - 6-12 month lead times for new production capacity
   - Essential for high-performance batteries (natural graphite has inferior performance)

2. **LFP Cathode Materials**:
   - China dominates production (CATL, BYD, Gotion, etc.)
   - Patent landscape and manufacturing know-how concentrated in China
   - Cost advantages due to integrated supply chain

3. **Production Equipment**:
   - Chinese equipment manufacturers (Wuxi Lead, Yinghe Technology, etc.) dominate mid-market equipment
   - Controls limit ability to establish new battery production outside China

**Affected Industries:**

**1. Electric Vehicle Manufacturing:**
- Global automakers dependent on Chinese battery supply chains
- Tesla, VW, GM, Ford, Mercedes, BMW all have Chinese battery dependencies
- Delays in battery supply = delays in EV production targets

**2. Energy Storage Systems:**
- Grid-scale storage projects (renewable energy integration)
- Residential and commercial battery storage
- Telecommunications backup power

**3. Consumer Electronics:**
- Smartphones, laptops, tablets
- Power tools, e-bikes, drones
- Portable electronics

**4. Aerospace and Defense:**
- Electric aircraft and UAV development
- Military vehicle electrification
- Portable power systems for defense applications

**Strategic Implications:**

1. **Energy Transition Vulnerability**:
   - Global electrification goals depend on lithium battery supply chains
   - Controls create leverage over countries' decarbonization efforts
   - Potential delays to climate targets if battery supply is restricted

2. **Industrial Policy Response**:
   - Accelerated push for domestic battery supply chains (U.S. IRA, EU Battery Regulation)
   - Increased investment in alternative chemistries (sodium-ion, solid-state)
   - Strategic stockpiling of critical materials and equipment

3. **Technology Divergence**:
   - May accelerate development of non-Chinese battery technologies
   - Risk of global battery market fragmentation
   - Duplication of R&D and capital investment

4. **Licensing Process Impact**:
   - Lead time delays for equipment procurement
   - Uncertainty for long-term supply contracts
   - Potential denials for strategic/military end-uses

**Specific Vulnerabilities:**

- **300 Wh/kg Battery Threshold**: Captures most modern EV batteries (typical range: 250-350 Wh/kg at pack level, higher at cell level)
- **Graphitization Technology**: Extremely difficult to replicate due to high energy costs and specialized know-how
- **LFP ≥2.5 g/cm³**: Targets high-quality LFP suitable for fast-charging EVs (lower density LFP may be less restricted)

**Geographic Impact:**

- **Europe**: Heavy dependence on Chinese battery materials and equipment for EU Green Deal targets
- **United States**: Despite IRA incentives, still dependent on Chinese supply chains (especially graphite)
- **Japan/South Korea**: Domestic battery industries dependent on Chinese raw materials and equipment
- **India**: Emerging EV market highly dependent on Chinese battery ecosystem
- **Southeast Asia**: Battery manufacturing hubs (Thailand, Indonesia) rely on Chinese materials and equipment
