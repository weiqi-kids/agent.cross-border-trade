## Summary

The 32nd Asian Export Control Seminar was held in Tokyo on February 3-5, hosted by Japan's Center for Information on Security Trade Control (CISTEC) and co-organized by Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI) and Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA). Around 130 delegates from over 30 countries/regions, international organizations, and multilateral export-control regimes participated, discussing intangible technology transfer, exporter compliance, and strengthening national export-licensing review systems.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority / Organizers**: Center for Information on Security Trade Control (日本安全保障贸易情报中心, CISTEC), co-organized by Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (经济产业省, METI) and Ministry of Foreign Affairs (外务省, MOFA)
- **Effective Date**: Event held 2026-02-03 to 2026-02-05, Tokyo
- **Scope**: Multilateral export-control cooperation; ~130 delegates from 30+ countries/regions (13 from Asia), international organizations, and multilateral export-control regimes

## Key Provisions

- Three main discussion topics:
  1. Appropriate transfer of intangible technology (无形技术的适当转让).
  2. Strengthening exporter compliance (加强出口商合规性).
  3. Expanding national/regional export-licensing review systems (扩充各国和地区的出口许可审查制度).
- These were identified as common challenges facing global export control.
- Japan's METI stated it will continue exchange activities with Asian countries and support other countries in building and improving export-control systems.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*以下為推測性分析，僅供參考。* Continued multilateral coordination, particularly on intangible technology transfer and licensing review, points toward gradually tightening and more harmonized export-control practices across Asia. Exporters of dual-use and sensitive technologies in the region may face increasingly aligned compliance expectations, raising the importance of robust internal control programs.
