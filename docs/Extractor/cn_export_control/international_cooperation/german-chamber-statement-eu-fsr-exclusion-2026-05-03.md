## Summary

The German Chamber of Commerce in China (德国中国商会) issued a statement responding to the European Commission's decision under the Foreign Subsidies Regulation (《外国补贴条例》, FSR) regarding a Lisbon metro project, in which a Chinese subcontractor was required to be replaced on the grounds of "distortive subsidies." The Chamber warned that the decision's impact extends well beyond a single procurement project and creates a demonstration effect, raising compliance costs and uncertainty for Chinese firms participating in EU projects.

## Key Provisions

- The EU Commission required replacement of a Chinese subcontractor on the Lisbon metro project, citing "distortive subsidies" (扭曲性补贴).
- The Chamber raised three core concerns:
  1. **Spillover effect (外溢效应)** — Chinese firms in Germany already feel negative impacts.
  2. **Business-environment risk (商业环境风险)** — the enforcement trend raises "uncertainty and compliance costs" for participating in EU projects, threatening long-term operations.
  3. **Investment confidence (投资信心问题)** — measures may "weaken foreign investors' confidence in the EU market."
- The Chamber called on the EU to clarify enforcement standards, improve rule stability and predictability, and maintain a fair competitive environment.
