## Summary

Finland's Ministry of Foreign Affairs is drafting a government proposal to revise its Dual-Use Items Export Control Law (《两用物项出口管制法》, Law 500/2024). The revision aligns the national control list with the EU Export Control Regulation (EU 2021/821), removes items already covered by the EU control list, accommodates new national general export licenses, and clarifies reporting obligations. A public consultation on the final proposal is expected in late June or early July 2026.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Finnish Ministry of Foreign Affairs (芬兰外交部)
- **Effective Date**: Not yet enacted; public consultation expected late June / early July 2026 (training session held 2026-06-02; registration deadline 2026-05-29)
- **Scope**: National dual-use export control framework; alignment with EU Export Control Regulation (EU 2021/821); introduction of unmanned drone technology into the national control list

## Key Provisions

- Remove items already listed in the EU control list from the national control list (targeted by November 2025).
- Update the law to accommodate new national general export licenses (NGELs).
- Clarify reporting obligations for exporters.
- Technical amendments to Sections 8 and 9 of the Law.
- Introduce unmanned drone (UAV) technology into the national control framework to address public safety risks.
- Eliminate administrative overlap between national and EU-level regulations while preserving existing business obligations.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*以下為推測性分析，僅供參考。* Streamlining the Finnish national list against the EU list should reduce duplicate licensing burden for EU-based dual-use exporters operating through Finland. The addition of UAV technology to the national list may create new licensing requirements for drone component and technology suppliers. Exporters of EU-listed dual-use items should monitor the late-June/early-July public consultation to assess the final scope before the law takes effect.
