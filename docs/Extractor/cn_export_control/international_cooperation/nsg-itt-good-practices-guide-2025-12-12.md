## Summary

The Nuclear Suppliers Group (NSG, 核供应国集团) released a "Good Practices Guide for Implementing NSG Intangible Technology Transfer (ITT) Control Measures" (《关于实施核供应国集团无形技术转让（ITT）管控措施的良好实践指南》). The guide describes best practices for applying controls to intangible transfers of NSG-listed sensitive nuclear-related technology, aiming to prevent unauthorized transfers.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Nuclear Suppliers Group (NSG)
- **Effective Date**: Not specified (guidance document)
- **Scope**: Intangible technology transfer (ITT) of NSG-controlled nuclear and nuclear-related dual-use technology

## Key Provisions

- Introduces best-practice approaches for implementing control measures over intangible technology transfers (ITT) — e.g., transfers via email, cloud, remote access, training, and oral disclosure.
- Targets NSG-listed sensitive technologies to prevent unauthorized or proliferation-sensitive transfers.
- Advisory framework for NSG participating governments and exporters.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*Speculative analysis:* The guide is non-binding but signals tightening international norms around intangible (non-physical) transfers of nuclear-related technology. Firms and research institutions involved in nuclear, nuclear dual-use, and high-technology collaboration may need to strengthen internal compliance programs covering remote access, cloud storage, and technical training across borders. Direct, immediate supply chain disruption is unlikely.
