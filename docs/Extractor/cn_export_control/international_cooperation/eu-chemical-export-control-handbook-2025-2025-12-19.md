## Summary

The European Union released the 2025 edition (fourth version) of its Chemical Export Control Handbook (《化学品出口管制手册》). The handbook covers approximately 2,100 chemical substances subject to export restrictions or sanctions prohibitions, providing technical guidance for exporters navigating complex regulatory frameworks while reducing the proliferation of toxic chemicals and precursors.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: European Union (European Commission)
- **Effective Date**: 2025 (handbook publication; no specific enforcement date)
- **Scope**: Approximately 2,100 controlled chemical substances; EU exporters of chemicals and precursors

## Key Provisions

- Updated technical guidance handbook for chemical exporters (fourth version / 2025 edition).
- Regulatory basis: the Chemical Weapons Convention (CWC) and multiple EU regulations.
- Covers ~2,100 chemical substances under export restrictions or sanctions prohibitions.
- Purpose: help exporters comply with complex regulatory frameworks and curb proliferation of toxic chemicals and precursors.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*Speculative analysis:* Chinese chemical exporters and global firms trading with EU counterparties may face increased compliance documentation requirements when dealing with the ~2,100 listed substances. Firms in fine chemicals, pharmaceutical intermediates, and precursor supply chains should review whether their products fall within the updated list. The guidance is advisory in nature rather than a new binding control list, so direct trade disruption is likely limited.
