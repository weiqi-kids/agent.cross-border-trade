## Summary

Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (经济产业省, METI) revised the Export Trade Control Order (《出口贸易管理令》). Effective August 18, 2025, the revision—stemming from an amendment to Japan's Self-Defense Forces Law—changes references in Annex 1, Section 1 from "Australian military" to "treaty nation military," and clarifies the end-use (catch-all) circumstances under which exporters must assess whether goods may be used for developing, manufacturing, or using controlled items.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry, Japan (经济产业省 / METI)
- **Effective Date**: 2025-08-18
- **Scope**: Dual-use export categories under Articles 4(1)(3) and 4(1)(4) of the Export Trade Control Order (excluding nuclear weapons classifications)

## Key Provisions

- Amends Annex 1, Section 1: replaces references to "Australian military" with "treaty nation military," reflecting Japan's Self-Defense Forces Law amendment.
- Clarifies catch-all (end-use) control scenarios in which exporters must determine whether goods may be used for development, manufacture, or use of controlled items:
  1. Initial exports — when contracts/documents indicate intended use in controlled development.
  2. Ongoing development — when evidence shows the importer is currently developing controlled items.
  3. Completed development — when evidence shows the importer has already developed controlled items.
- Exporters must examine export-related contracts, acquired documentation, and importer notifications to determine applicability.
- Applies to categories under Articles 4(1)(3) and 4(1)(4); excludes nuclear weapons classifications.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*Speculative analysis:* The clarified catch-all (end-use) screening obligations increase due-diligence requirements for Japanese exporters of dual-use goods, who must more carefully assess importer development activities across the export lifecycle. Firms in defense-adjacent, advanced manufacturing, and dual-use technology supply chains trading with Japan should strengthen end-use verification and documentation. The "treaty nation military" reclassification reflects evolving Japanese defense partnerships rather than a direct trade restriction.
