## Summary

The Japanese government passed a Cabinet decision formally revising the "Three Principles on Transfer of Defense Equipment" (《防卫装备转移三原则》) and its implementation guidelines, shifting policy from "in principle prohibited" to "in principle permitted" for the export of finished weapons, including lethal weapons. The revision removes the prior restriction limiting exports to five categories of non-combat purposes and allows exceptions for transfers to countries experiencing conflict.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Government of Japan (日本政府), via Cabinet decision
- **Effective Date**: Not specified in source (Cabinet decision dated on or around 2026-04-21)
- **Scope**: Export of finished weapons including lethal weapons; defense equipment transfer framework

## Key Provisions

- Revises the "Three Principles on Transfer of Defense Equipment" and implementation guidelines.
- Removes the prior "non-combat use" limitation (previously restricted to five categories of non-combat purposes).
- Permits export of finished weapons (成品武器), including lethal weapons (杀伤性武器).
- Allows exceptions for weapons transfers to countries experiencing conflict.
- Approval process: Prime Minister, Chief Cabinet Secretary, Foreign Minister, and Defense Minister approve exports.
- The Diet receives post-approval notifications rather than prior consultation.
- Represents the culmination of gradual restriction removals since 2006, transforming policy from "原则禁止" (principle of prohibition) to "原则允许" (principle of permission).
