## Summary

The United Kingdom announced "The Export Control (Amendment) (No. 2) Regulations 2025" (《2025年出口管制（修订）（第2号）条例》) on November 20, 2025, amending the Export Control Order 2008. The amendments update military items (Schedule 2), remove three dual-use entries (PL9013/9014/9015) from Schedule 3, and reclassify Armenia and Azerbaijan in the destinations schedule (Schedule 4). The changes take effect on December 16, 2025.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: United Kingdom (Government / Export Control Joint Unit)
- **Effective Date**: 2025-12-16 (announced 2025-11-20)
- **Scope**: Military items, dual-use goods (quantum/cryogenic/semiconductor/additive manufacturing/advanced materials), and destination controls

## Key Provisions

- **Schedule 2 (Military Items)**: Added suborbital vehicle definitions and updated technical descriptions in category ML7h.
- **Schedule 3 (Dual-use Goods)**: Removed three items — PL9013, PL9014, PL9015 — transferred to EU Council Regulation 428/2009. These items (originally introduced in 2024) covered quantum technology, cryogenic technology, semiconductor technology, additive manufacturing equipment, and advanced materials.
- **Schedule 4 (Destinations)**: Reclassified Armenia and Azerbaijan from military embargo status to Category B goods requiring transit controls only.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*Speculative analysis:* The removal of national PL9013-9015 controls (now aligned to the EU regulation reference) may streamline UK dual-use licensing for quantum, cryogenic, semiconductor, additive manufacturing, and advanced materials exporters. The addition of suborbital vehicle definitions signals tightening of aerospace/space-related military controls. The reclassification of Armenia and Azerbaijan eases certain destination restrictions. Firms exporting from or via the UK in these categories should review the December 16, 2025 effective changes.
