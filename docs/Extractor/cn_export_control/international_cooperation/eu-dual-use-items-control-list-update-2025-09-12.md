## Summary

The European Commission adopted a delegated regulation updating Annex I of EU Regulation 2021/821 (《欧盟两用物项出口管制条例》, the 2021 EU Dual-Use Items Export Control Regulation) on September 8, 2025. The revision aligns the EU dual-use control list with the latest control lists of the four major multilateral export control regimes, adding entries for quantum technology, semiconductor manufacturing/testing equipment, advanced computing integrated circuits, high-temperature coatings, additive manufacturing, and peptide synthesizers.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: European Commission (European Union)
- **Effective Date**: Adopted 2025-09-08 (delegated regulation)
- **Scope**: Dual-use items (两用物项) — quantum, semiconductor, advanced computing, advanced materials, additive manufacturing, and biotechnology sectors

## Key Provisions

- Revises Annex I of EU Regulation 2021/821 to maintain consistency with the latest control lists of the four major multilateral export control regimes (Wassenaar Arrangement, NSG, MTCR, Australia Group).
- **New/added controls**: quantum technology; semiconductor manufacturing and testing equipment and materials; advanced computing integrated circuits and electronic components; high-temperature coatings; additive manufacturing equipment and related materials; peptide synthesizers.
- Updated certain control parameters and modernized technical definitions and descriptions for existing entries.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*Speculative analysis:* The addition of quantum, semiconductor manufacturing/testing, advanced computing ICs, and additive manufacturing entries broadens EU licensing requirements for high-technology exports and may affect firms in the semiconductor equipment, advanced computing, advanced materials, and biotechnology supply chains trading with or through the EU. Chinese and global suppliers should assess whether their products fall within the newly added categories and prepare for additional licensing scrutiny.
