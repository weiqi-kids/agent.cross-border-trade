## Summary

The Wassenaar Arrangement (瓦森纳安排, WA) Plenary revised and clarified its dual-use items and technologies control list at its 29th Plenary Meeting held in Vienna on December 3-4, 2025. The revisions added or clarified controls on lasers, imaging sensors and cameras, subsea survey equipment, parachutes/paragliders, and ceramic composite materials, while removing certain items no longer deemed militarily significant.

## Key Details

- **Issuing Authority**: Wassenaar Arrangement (WA) Plenary
- **Effective Date**: Adopted at 29th WA Plenary, Vienna, December 3-4, 2025
- **Scope**: Dual-use items (两用物项) and technologies across lasers, sensors, marine survey, aerospace, and advanced materials sectors

## Key Provisions

- **Revised/clarified controls** on: lasers (including those used in automotive, shipbuilding, and aerospace industries), imaging sensors and cameras, subsea survey equipment, parachutes and paragliders, and ceramic composite materials.
- **Removed items**: certain temperature sensors and underwater visual/lighting systems, now considered no longer militarily significant.
- Updated the WA Best Practice Guidelines for internal compliance programs (ICP) for dual-use items and technologies.
- Revisions reflect international security developments, technological change, and market trends, promoting transparency in conventional arms and dual-use technology transfers.

## Supply Chain Impact Assessment

*Speculative analysis:* As the WA control list informs the export-control regimes of 42 participating states (including major technology exporters), the additions in lasers, imaging sensors, and ceramic composites may broaden licensing requirements affecting automotive, shipbuilding, and aerospace supply chains. Conversely, the removal of certain temperature sensors and underwater lighting systems may ease trade in those categories. Firms sourcing or exporting these dual-use items should monitor national implementation in WA member states.
